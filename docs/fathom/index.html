CHALLENGER DEEP

10,935 m · 1,095 atmospheres · 1.8°C

The deepest known point in Earth's ocean. The anchor rests here now, in sediment fine as flour, where the water pressure is eight tons per square inch and the nearest sunlight is a seven-mile swim straight up.

Three crewed expeditions have touched this floor:

1960 · Trieste — Jacques Piccard & Don Walsh
2012 · Deepsea Challenger — James Cameron, alone
2019— · DSV Limiting Factor — Victor Vescovo & crews

One file. No frameworks. No WebGL.

Everything you just saw is a single HTML document and the browser's own machinery:

  • animation-timeline: scroll() — the water darkens through oklch color space, driven by the scrollbar itself, off the main thread.
  • animation-timeline: view() — every creature, marker, and zone title reveals itself the moment it enters your viewport. Zero scroll listeners for the visuals.
  • offset-path — the fish school swims along a CSS motion path.
  • linear() easing — the jellyfish pulse on a hand-tuned spring curve.
  • Popover API + @starting-style — these fact cards, with native top-layer rendering and entry transitions.
  • Web Audio API — the drone, whale call, sonar ping, and hull creaks are synthesized live from oscillators and noise. There are no audio files.
  • Canvas 2D — the bioluminescent plankton that follow your pointer in the dark.
  • SVG — all 24 inhabitants, hand-drawn.
