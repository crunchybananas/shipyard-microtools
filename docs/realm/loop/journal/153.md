## why this shape

- **120's argument.** Integer damage makes a +5%
  bonus invisible at most raider HPs. Raider HPs
  cluster at 10/15/20/30; +5% damage (10→10.5)
  doesn't cross any kill threshold. Fire-rate
  changes the INTER-SHOT TIME — continuous, no
  thresholding.
- **Same ~5% DPS magnitude.** Smith bonus maintains
  parity with teacher (+10% research, 101) and
  merchant (+5% trade, 102). The 5% feels right for
  graduated named-character mechanics — 120 audit
  confirmed cumulative 3-char speedup of ~25% on
  a 200-day realm was balanced (not game-breaking,
  not invisible).
- **Non-integer fireTimer is fine.** `fireTimer`
  decrements by `G.speed` per tick — float value
  works. 57.142... ticks between shots vs 60 gives
  exactly 5% more shots over time.
- **Sibling-pattern preserved.** 101/102's `const
  <char>Mult = G.namedCharacters?.<char> ? 1.N : 1`
  boilerplate is intact; just applied to the
  inverse of fireTimer instead of the damage
  field.
- **No UI.** 101/102/105 were all silent mechanics
  (no chronicle, no UI indicator). 153 keeps that.
  The difference reads as subtle "combat feels a
  bit snappier when smith is named" — discoverable
  by observation, not telegraphed.

## the balance math

Current: fireTimer = 60, damage = 10 → DPS = 10 per
60 ticks = 1/6 damage per tick per tower.

Before 153 (smith): damage = 10.5 → DPS = 10.5/60
= 0.175. Kill-threshold examples:
- HP 10: 1 shot (unchanged; threshold crossed at
  damage ≥ 10)
- HP 15: 2 shots (10.5 * 2 = 21 ≥ 15) → 2 shots
  (unchanged)
- HP 20: 2 shots (10.5 * 2 = 21 ≥ 20) → 2 shots
  (unchanged, was already 2 at damage 10)
- HP 21: 2 shots (was 3 at damage 10; **CHANGED**)
- HP 30: 3 shots (was 3 at damage 10; unchanged)

So the damage bonus only helped in a narrow HP
window (21, 31-33, etc). Most raiders missed the
benefit.

After 153 (smith): fireTimer = 57.14, damage = 10
→ DPS = 10/57.14 = 0.175 (same magnitude). Kill
times ALWAYS ~5% faster regardless of HP. The
bonus manifests on every engagement.

Scaling: 2 towers each killing a 30-HP raider takes
3 shots × 60 ticks = 180 ticks pre-smith, vs 3 × 57
= 171 ticks post-smith. Savings of ~9 ticks = ~0.15s
at speed=1 per raider. Multiplied across 40 raiders
per realm (120 numbers): ~6 seconds shaved. Still
within the 25% cumulative budget.
