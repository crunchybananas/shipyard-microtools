## baseline voice

**Register:** rural-medieval, lightly archaic. Collective-
subjective (the realm, the settlers, we). Present tense for
events. Short declarative sentences ~10-25 words, often
two-beat (first sentence states, second sentence reflects).
Occasional em-dash or semicolon for rhythm. Sparing imagery;
concrete verbs preferred ("quenched", "pressed", "stands")
over abstract ("was made", "occurred"). No exclamations in
90%+ of prose.

**Dark register** (nightmare, requiem, stone, happiness-
crisis) uses the same voice but with uncanny composition:
bell tolls itself, every dog bark at same star, last fire
goes out.

**Personification range:** low in BUILDING_FIRST_BEATS
("the mill hums"), high in RESEARCH_BEATS ("coins begin
to remember which hands they've passed through"). Both
fit the register; different ticks' authors lean different
ways.

## HIGH drift — castleBuilt uses quoted dialogue

`NARRATIVE_BEATS` entry for castleBuilt:

```js
text: G => {
  const m = G.namedCharacters.mayor;
  return `The castle stands complete. ${m ? m.name + ' proclaims,' : 'The heralds proclaim,'} "${G.kingdomName} shall endure a thousand years!"`;
}
```

Renders as:

> "The castle stands complete. Mayor Osric proclaims, '`TestRealm` shall endure a thousand years!'"

**This is the only chronicle entry in the game with:**
- Quoted direct speech (`"…"`)
- Exclamation mark inside the prose

**Why it reads wrong:**
- Every other beat speaks ABOUT the realm or its people;
  this one speaks AS them.
- The quoted speech shifts from narrator-observer voice
  to theatrical-proclamation voice. Jarring against the
  quiet lift of neighboring milestone beats.
- Exclamation mark amplifies the shift.

**Fix draft:**

```js
text: G => {
  const m = G.namedCharacters.mayor;
  return `The castle stands complete. ${m ? m.name : 'The heralds'} name it to endure a thousand years; the realm takes the name.`;
}
```

- Removes quotation marks
- Removes exclamation
- Preserves "thousand years" phrase + mayor-name conditional
- Adds "the realm takes the name" phrasing that echoes
  116's constellation beat ("The realm takes the name.")
  — creates a deliberate callback

## LOW drift — pop-50 exclamation

`NARRATIVE_BEATS.pop50`:

> "Fifty strong! Travelers on the road whisper of the
>   rising realm."

**Only non-castleBuilt exclamation in the prose corpus.**
pop-10/25/75/100 all end with periods:
- "Ten souls now call this land home."
- "Twenty-five citizens — a true town takes shape."
- "Seventy-five subjects. Other lords now watch with envy."
- "One hundred souls. A city is born."

**Fix draft:** "Fifty strong. Travelers on the road
whisper of the rising realm."

One-character change. Restores consistency.

## LOW drift — pop-75 perspective shift

> "Seventy-five subjects. Other lords now watch with envy."

"Other lords" introduces OUTSIDE the realm's viewpoint.
Most beats stay inside (the realm, the settlers, the
elders). Only three explicit outside-viewpoint beats:
- pop-75 (other lords)
- pop-50 (travelers on the road)
- First-raid-survived "The dead are buried; the living
  drink to the fallen" — actually stays inside; no drift

pop-50 + pop-75 both bring in outside-observer. Mild
drift but **this is arguably intentional** — population
milestones are ABOUT being noticed externally. I don't
recommend changing. Flag only.

## NOTE — RESEARCH_BEATS has stronger personification

Compare:

BUILDING_FIRST_BEATS mine:
> "Picks ring in the iron mine. Ore carts clatter up
> from the dark."
(action verbs; mine does not "know" anything)

RESEARCH_BEATS masonry:
> "The realm masters masonry. Stone now speaks in lines
> and courses."
(stone itself SPEAKS; object has agency)

BUILDING_FIRST_BEATS windmill:
> "Sails turn above the fields. Grain will become flour
> now, without the miller's back."
(action + implication)

RESEARCH_BEATS engineering:
> "Engineering is grasped. Water runs where it is told;
> the roads remember where they lead."
(water obeys; roads remember)

RESEARCH_BEATS chose a deliberately more mystical voice
("stone speaks", "coins remember", "thresholds know") —
knowledge-as-sentience framing. This is a distinct register
WITHIN the broader voice, not drift. Tick 065's journal
called this out explicitly. Keep as-is.

## what felt the most EARNED

Subjectively, the most earned prose in the corpus:

1. **Subsequent-winter beats (093)** — the founder1-aging
   arc. "Lira knows what to store" → "counts them like
   elders" → "remembers the first, and speaks of it less
   often now." Earned because it composes across 4 ticks
   (088 + 093×3) with the same canonical character.

2. **Requiem (103)** — "The last fire in the realm goes
   out. ${f}'s name is the last spoken, and then there is
   no one to speak it." Earned because the realm's
   death-silence gap was 100-ticks-filed; the prose meets
   the weight.

3. **Happiness-crisis (071)** — "A grey mood settles
   over the realm. Doors close earlier; the bards hold
   their tongues." Quietly specific; "bards hold their
   tongues" does more than "people are unhappy."

## what felt the most FORCED

1. **castleBuilt** — (see HIGH above)

2. **pop-100** — "One hundred souls. A city is born."
   Technically correct but feels like a placeholder. Other
   pop milestones have more specific imagery. A rewrite
   could use the named-kingdom: "One hundred souls. The
   name ${G.kingdomName} carries farther than it did at
   ten." Filed idea.

3. **099's winter-dust farm comment** (not prose per se;
   mentioning as contrast): the code comments have been
   more literary in the past few ticks than some of the
   older placeholder beats. That's fine — just means the
   beats deserve occasional refresh passes.

## honest meta

The prose overall is in a strong consistent voice.
**Both HIGH and LOW drift candidates are isolated to
pre-090 code** — the castleBuilt text came from the
pre-refactor era, the pop-50 exclamation likewise. Every
post-090 tick's prose (winter arc, founder fragments,
dream variants, offering, requiem, constellation) is in
voice.

The 4 drift items found are LESS than I expected in a
150-entry corpus authored across 100+ ticks. Credit: the
invariant that "no tag contention, tag-reuse over
proliferation" (075) also unconsciously enforced voice
alignment — reusing an existing tag meant looking at
existing prose, which propagated the voice.
