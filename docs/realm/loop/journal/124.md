## the build

Three coordinated changes.

### 1. `docs/realm/js/audio.js` — new `first-snow` case

```js
case 'first-snow': {
  makeNoiseBurst(ctx, dest, 0.04, 0.9, t, 8000);
  makeNoiseBurst(ctx, dest, 0.025, 0.7, t + 0.15, 6000);
  // Single high sine — like a distant bell at the frost threshold.
  makeOsc(ctx, dest, 'sine', 1568, 0.02, 0.02, 1.2, t + 0.05);
  break;
}
```

**Sound design — whispering-coded:**
- **Two overlapping noise bursts** at 8 kHz + 6 kHz cut
  frequencies, 150ms stagger. Primary burst 0.9s with
  gain 0.04; secondary 0.7s at 0.025. Produces a
  shimmer that sounds like snow landing on the
  periphery of hearing.
- **Single high sine** at 1568 Hz (G6) — distant bell
  overtone. Very quiet (0.02). Gives the texture a
  tiny tonal anchor without breaking the noise-based
  character.
- **Non-tonal texture** deliberately breaks the pure-
  sine pattern established by 111/113/115. The catalog
  now has 4 tonal cues + 1 noise-based. First-snow is
  distinct because the IMAGE is distinct: snow isn't a
  pitched event, it's a texture.
- **Gain 0.04 peak** — quietest cue in the catalog
  (111 was 0.08, 115 was 0.06). Deliberate: snow should
  be barely audible, matching "miss-able" invariant.

## why this shape

- **Noise-based breaks the pure-sine pattern
  deliberately.** The first 4 cues were all tonal.
  Snow isn't pitched. Matching the image to the
  texture matters more than acoustic uniformity.
- **Overlapping bursts create shimmer** without
  requiring a true stereo effect. The 150ms stagger
  gives the ear a sense of "multiple flakes" instead
  of "single gust."
- **High-freq sine + noise** mixes textures within
  one cue. 113's stone chime had a shimmer overtone
  (B6 at 1976 Hz); 124's first-snow goes a step
  further — the overtone is the ONLY tonal element,
  framing the noise.
- **Quietest in catalog (0.04 peak).** The "miss-able"
  invariant gets its strongest test here. A player
  who isn't listening closely will register first-snow
  as wind or ambient noise; a player WHO IS listening
  will hear the 1568 Hz sine as a faint bell.
- **5th cue; 1st non-tonal.** Contrast table's
  `Structure` axis now has an "overlapping bursts"
  entry — no prior cue had this shape. Each cue
  continues to occupy distinct coordinates.
