# 013 — the-screenshot-critic

**Challenge:** the-screenshot-critic — accepted: capture
5+ game states, pick the weakest frame, say why. Secondary:
verify 012's hue-varied tint reads distinguishably — the
original 003 "winter midday vs dusk look identical"
complaint is the acid test.

## frames captured (6 total)

1. **Summer midday** (`t=0.50`) — overlay OFF. Tiles pure
   summer-tileShift. Vibrant greens, golden sand, inky-teal
   water. Bright but flat (no tint variation is possible
   here; daylight=1.0).
2. **Summer dawn** (`t=0.05`) — soft rose tint. Grass reads
   muted-warm, water reads warm-teal (not blue). Subtle but
   present. **Not a strong frame** — the rose window is so
   narrow and the darkness so shallow that the rose barely
   distinguishes from the ambient warm summer palette.
3. **Summer dusk** (`t=0.70`) — warm amber tint at peak.
   Grass olive-warm, sand golden-orange, water warm-teal.
   **Best frame captured today.** Reads as genuine sunset.
   Strong candidate for a cover-art-adjacent screenshot.
4. **Summer night** (`t=0.95`) — cool indigo tint.
   Navy-green tiles, deep blue water, sparkles in sky.
   Clearly distinct from summer dusk. Classic night vibe,
   legible (night floor holds).
5a. **Winter midday** (`t=0.50`) — overlay OFF. Tiles
    winter-tileShift only. Muted blue-green grass, grey
    stone, less-saturated water. Drained feel.
5b. **Winter dusk** (`t=0.70`) — warm amber tint over
    winter-tileShifted tiles. The amber partially
    **neutralizes** the cool tileShift — grass reads
    olive-khaki, sand golden, water less-blue. **Clearly
    distinct from 5a.** The 003 complaint is resolved.

## the acid test

003's original complaint: "winter midday and winter dusk
read as the same tonal value." Side-by-side 5a and 5b now:

- 5a: cool-muted palette. No warmth anywhere.
- 5b: warm amber wash over same cool tiles. Grass pulled
  toward olive, sand toward gold, silhouettes warmer.

These are two legibly-different times of day in winter for
the first time since the curve was authored. **012 holds
up in the wild.** The structural fix (hue-varying the tint
overlay) worked where 005's proposed luminance re-shape
would not have.

## weakest frame: 5a — Winter midday

Winter midday gets NO tint because daylight=1.0 and the
overlay turns off. What's left is the bare
`SEASONS.winter.tileShift = [-10, -5, +15]` applied to
every tile:

- R drops by 10 (kills warmth)
- G drops by 5 (pulls green toward grey)
- B rises by 15 (adds blue cast)

The result is a **uniformly blue-washed flat-bright
landscape**. No highlight, no shadow direction, no
temperature shift — just a cold even overcast. Compare
Summer midday (Frame 1) which has saturated contrast.

Why this is now the weakest:

- **012 fixed dusk/night/dawn but left midday alone.**
  Midday is where no tint applies (daylight plateau). If
  winter midday feels bad, 012 can't help — the fix is in
  the tileShift or a season-aware midday path.
- **Winter-dusk (5b) is now visibly warm** — better than
  winter-midday. That's the reverse of the usual
  expectation (midday should be the brightest, warmest
  moment). Winter in realm now has its *best* moment at
  dusk, not noon. Unusual, borderline wrong.
- **Midday is the majority of the "active play" time**.
  Players spend 42% of every day in the flat plateau at
  daylight=1.0. Whatever midday feels like dominates the
  player's memory of a season.

Fix direction (not done this tick): adjust
`SEASONS.winter.tileShift`. Current `[-10, -5, +15]` is
too cold. Proposals:

- Shift toward warm-neutral: `[-5, -3, +8]` — same
  direction, half magnitude.
- Introduce a highlight channel: keep tint shift but add a
  sun-warm-pass that adds +5/+3/0 to lit tiles.
- Accept "cold flat winter" as intentional and address by
  moving the sun angle (impossible within current arch)
  or adding lit-window glow on buildings at winter midday.

## what 012 does well (worth preserving)

- Dusk is the game's new strongest frame. Deliberately
  going to t=0.70 is a way to get a cover-art moment.
- Night is no longer just "dark summer" — the indigo cast
  gives night its own palette.
- Dawn, while subtle, IS different from dusk. Narrow
  window but present.
- No visible boundary pop at any dayT transition (dawn
  ends where overlay turns off; amber→indigo lerp is
  continuous).

## other findings from this session

- **Live season override via `G.season='winter'` still
  doesn't update HUD** (003 finding, not resolved). HUD
  said "Summer" throughout the winter frames — game
  engine's season state lives elsewhere from `G.season`
  key. Non-blocking for photography but worth a future
  `G.setSeason()` helper.
- **The "PAUSED" label center-top remains a composition
  intrusion** in every paused screenshot (idea 003 —
  photo-mode with UI-hide — still open).

## ideas generated → ideas.md

- Winter tileShift re-tune: `[-10, -5, +15]` is too cold
  on its own; consider `[-5, -3, +8]` or add a warmth-
  compensating midday mode for winter.
- Season-aware midday tint: introduce a very-light tint
  even at `daylight=1.0` that reflects the season (subtle
  warm in summer, subtle cool in winter, etc.). This
  makes midday participate in the day-color system
  instead of being the only frame without chromatic
  season coupling.
