## why this shape

- **Closes the stone arc deliberately.** Four
  beats now make a complete narrative cycle:
  - 056 (a-scene-that-happens-once): discovery.
    "A standing stone is found at dawn..."
  - 079 (the-cartographer or similar): the
    offering at the stone — cross-system gated
    on `stone_found && happyPeakActive`.
  - 122 (surprise): the stone weathers — 4
    kingdom-hashed variants of erosion /
    settling / nesting after day ≥ 150.
  - **184 (this): the stone is forgotten.**
    Realm-elders-don't-talk-about-it.
- **Generational-gap framing matches 147.**
  147 great-storm uses "children born after do
  not believe it" — past-event-not-witnessed.
  184 stone uses "youngest in the realm have
  never heard the story of who found it" — same
  past-event-not-witnessed structure but for an
  OBJECT instead of a WEATHER MEMORY. Two beats
  use the same generational-gap phrasing for
  two different focal axes.
- **Static text, no kingdom-hash variants.**
  Forgetting is universal — every realm forgets
  the same way (whereas weathering is per-
  kingdom-specific via 4 hash variants in 122).
  Restraint signal: the forgetting is uniform
  because the act of forgetting is universal.
- **Long-lived realm gating.** `year3` (day
  ≥ 57) + `stone_found` + `citizensBorn ≥ 10`.
  Year 3 is well past stone_found's discovery
  window (day 30-200). citizensBorn ≥ 10
  ensures meaningful intergenerational
  turnover. Most realms reach year 3; many will
  see this beat fire eventually.
- **Tag: stone (reuse).** 075 invariant. 085
  makes stone-tag eviction-immune so this beat
  survives chronicle cap pressure.
- **No founder reference.** Continues the
  157-policy preference (post-moratorium soft
  cap on founder surfaces). The stone arc has
  been founder-light from inception (056/079/
  122 all founder-free); 184 maintains that.
