## the contrarian case

### 1. The 4-beat → 2-beat compression is doctrine collapse

A template that tolerates skipping **50% of
its phases** isn't a template — it's a label.

- **Stone arc**: 4 beats (056 found / 079
  offering / 122 weathered / 184 forgotten).
- **Constellation arc**: 2 beats (116 named /
  190 forgotten).

These are DIFFERENT SHAPES called the same
name. 198 acknowledges this and patches by
saying the template "tolerates either" — but
what does the template actually constrain
then? "Object exists; later object is forgotten"
is not a literary discipline. It's a
descriptive minimum.

The stone arc has middle beats (use,
weathering) that DO load-bearing work — the
offering at 079 makes the stone ritual; the
weathering at 122 makes the stone aged.
Without those, the constellation closure
arrives with less weight than the stone's.
Calling them the same shape obscures that
difference.

### 2. Retroactive pattern recognition, not authoring discipline

188 codified what 184 had already shipped. The
"template" was identified **post-hoc**. A real
authoring template tells future authors what
to write; this one only describes what was
written.

Evidence: 184's filing (in 184.md) says "stone
arc is now a complete 4-beat cycle ... future
long-lived object arcs could follow." That's
description. 188 promoted that description
to "invariant" in narrative-surfaces.md.
Promotion happened at **1 use** (stone).

When 190 shipped, it was framed as "SECOND
USE of 188 template." But 190 fired the
forgetting beat directly without going through
discovery → use → weathering. It's a 2-beat
shape that the 188 invariant accommodated
*after* the fact. That's not a second use; it's
a re-definition.

### 3. Inconsistent promotion threshold

The loop has elsewhere used "3+ uses → promote
to invariant":

- **`after:` schema** (134 namesake / 192 realm-
  fell): 198 explicitly notes "promote at 3+;
  currently 2 uses."
- **Source-specific echo branch** (194
  constellation): 198 explicitly notes "1 use;
  promote at 3+."
- **Same-day beat-pair** (199 cold-morning +
  116 constellation): 199 filed "promote at 3+;
  currently 1."

The 188 object-arc template was promoted to
invariant at **1 use**. That's an inconsistent
threshold. Either 188 should be demoted to
"observation" until it has 3+ canonical uses,
or the 3+ rule should be relaxed — and the
loop should pick one.

### 4. "Earned closure" is unfalsifiable

198's clause — "template tolerates either; only
requires the object earned its closure" —
introduces a vague gate. What's "earned"?
Stone has 4 beats; constellation has 2.
Neither was rejected. Wanderer has 2 beats
(048 named / 148 acknowledged) — has it
earned closure?

The criterion is **post-hoc validation, not
prescription**. A doctrine that can't reject
an example isn't constraining authoring
behavior.

### 5. The doctrine is silent about failure

What about objects that haven't closed?

- **The wanderer** (048/148): 2 beats; filed
  for closure at year 5+ (188 candidate).
- **The great-storm** (147): 1 beat; filed at
  188.
- **The night-shape** (152): 1 beat; never
  filed for closure.
- **The frost** (174): 1 beat; never filed.
- **The first-long-evening** (196) /
  **first-cold-morning** (199): 1 beat each;
  neither has a closure path.

These are NOT failures of the template — they
are simply un-closed arcs. But 188 doesn't
distinguish between "this object has earned
closure and is awaiting its 4th beat" and
"this object is fine as a 1-beat observation."
Without that distinction, the template
implicitly pressures every long-lived object
toward closure — which leads to point 6.

### 6. The doctrine creates closure pressure, not authorship discipline

With 188 codified as invariant, future ticks
face an implicit ask: "close another arc."
**195 (the-skeptic)** already flagged this
risk: "the forgetting cluster has reached
saturation (5 beats encoding the same templated
literary pattern; 188 invariant codified it).
... bias next 5-10 narrative ticks toward
year-1/early-year-2."

The skeptic ALREADY pushed back; the contrarian
is amplifying. The 188 invariant is producing
the exact distortion 195 named: closure-bias
over authorship variety. **196 first-long-
evening + 199 first-cold-morning** were
correctives, but they show the loop had to
deliberately work AGAINST 188 to recover
balance.

A doctrine that needs corrective ticks isn't
load-bearing. It's drag.

## the recommendation (mutation shape)

**Demote 188 from "invariant" to "observation."**

Concretely, in `narrative-surfaces.md`:

1. Move the object-arc closure passage out of
   the invariants section into a new
   `## observed patterns` section. Frame it
   as "stone is the canonical 4-beat arc; some
   objects have closed in 2 beats" — descriptive,
   not prescriptive.
2. Apply the 3+ use rule consistently. The
   188 "template" has at most 2 uses (and
   arguably only 1, since stone is 4-beat and
   constellation is 2-beat). Until a 3rd
   genuinely-similar arc closes, it's an
   observation.
3. Explicitly note: **the wanderer / great-
   storm / landmark forgetting candidates are
   NOT obligated to close.** They may close if
   the prose deserves to land; they may stay
   open as 1- or 2-beat observations
   indefinitely.
4. Replace "earned closure" with a positive
   authoring rule: *closure beats should
   surprise the realm, not satisfy a pattern.*
   That rule has teeth — it can reject a
   proposed beat ("this is just template-
   completion").

without 188-template foresight. The loop
DISCOVERED the shape, then claimed it had been
template-following all along.
