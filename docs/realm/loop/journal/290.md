## why this surprises

This is the **FIRST INHERITANCE-VS-
CRAFT BEAT** in the corpus. All
previous land-as-agent uses observe
land/objects MADE BY OR BELONGING TO
the realm:
- 207 broad LAND collective ("the land
  has agreed to be lived in")
- 227 specific OBJECT (well) interiority
- 229 RELATIONAL keeper (hearth holds
  names)

290 introduces an **OBJECT FROM
ELSEWHERE** — the bell pulled from the
sea. The realm INHERITED it rather
than made it. And the lift line
acknowledges the inherited object
**outshines anything the realm itself
has cast**.

This is cultural HUMILITY about craft:
the realm acknowledges its found-
objects are sometimes better than its
made-objects. Different from forgetting
(184/190 = no longer remember WHO/WHAT)
because here the STORY is alive ("everyone
tells the story") and the object is in
ACTIVE USE ("still rings clearer").
