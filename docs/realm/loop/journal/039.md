## what shipped: collective dreams

Every 14 days at dawn, realm wakes to a shared dream.
A new chronicle entry appears composed from the
kingdom's current state: the buildings you've built,
the named characters you've met, and the name of the
kingdom itself. The same kingdom on the same day
always dreams the same dream (deterministic), but
two different kingdoms — or the same kingdom at a
later date — dream differently.

### the architecture (story.js:243+)

Eight dream "threads," each themed:

- **founding** — always available, rooted in boat-
  arrival imagery
- **forge** — unlocks when a smith is named
- **harvest** — unlocks when a farm is built
- **market** — unlocks when a merchant is named
- **warning** — unlocks when day > 20 or enemies
  present
- **hearth** — unlocks when a house exists
- **water** — unlocks when a well or fisherman exists
- **learning** — unlocks when a teacher is named

Each thread has 4–5 image fragments. Some threads
reach into `G.namedCharacters` for their text
(e.g., the forge thread names the specific smith:
*"Mira the Forger's anvil singing without a hammer"*).

The dream-builder:

1. On day % 14 === 0 at dawn (dayPhase ≤ 120), fires
   once per day (guarded by a storyFlag).
2. Picks 3 threads via per-slot hash
   (`kingdom_day_thread_i`), allowing repeats.
3. Picks one image per thread via its own per-slot
   hash (`kingdom_day_img_thread_i`).
4. Dedups by image text — early-game realms with
   only the `founding` thread still get distinct
   images from that pool.
5. Joins with `"; then "` into prose, wraps with
   framing:

   *"At dawn the realm wakes to shared dreams: [image];
   then [image]; then [image]. No one speaks of them,
   but everyone remembers."*

### verification (chrome-mcp)

Three different kingdoms on day 14 (all empty realms,
only founding thread):

- **Realm**: *"a drowned crown resting on sea-washed
  stones; then nets of starlight pulled in empty;
  then a boat of pale wood arriving at a shore that
  was not there yesterday."*
- **Avalonia**: *"three settlers at a fire that will
  not go out; then footprints in sand that the tide
  refuses; then a boat of pale wood arriving at a
  shore that was not there yesterday."*
- **Stonebridge**: *"footprints in sand that the tide
  refuses; then three settlers at a fire that will
  not go out; then a drowned crown resting on
  sea-washed stones."*

Rich realm at day 28 with smith + merchant + teacher
+ farm + house + well + fisherman + market + school +
blacksmith — threads expand:

*"ears of corn with the kingdom's name hidden in
their rows; then a merchant who trades only
questions, all of them the same; then a wolf at the
borders speaking the kingdom's founding words."*

And:

*"the alphabet of a tongue no one has yet spoken;
then three settlers at a fire that will not go out;
then steel poured like water, cooling into names no
one remembers."*

Rerunning `Realm_day28` with the same state produces
the identical dream (determinism preserved).

### a mid-tick bug I caught

First version dedupped threads before picking images.
Empty-realm runs (only 1 available thread) all
collapsed to 1 image, and even the 1-image output
collided across kingdoms when their hashes matched
mod 5 (pool length).

Fix: per-image hashing instead of shared seed
arithmetic. Now each image index derives from an
independent hash of the full key string. Entropy
scales with pool size, not with mod-of-pool collisions.

The first version would have been SHIPPED as a
surprise if I hadn't tested across kingdoms — the
journal would have read "great, it works for Realm"
and two days later a different-kingdom save would
have produced the exact same dream. Caught by running
the test with three distinct kingdoms in sequence.

## why this matters

The loop until now has mostly *fixed* things. This
tick invented a new narrative surface — one that
reacts to the player's realm state, names their
named characters, and produces prose no developer
wrote explicitly. In any given Peaceful game, the
player will experience roughly 5-8 dreams across a
typical session, each one unique to their realm.

The shape is also future-extensible: future ticks
can add threads (e.g., `seasons`, `raid_aftermath`,
`research`, `castle_built`) without touching any
other code — the builder pattern-matches existing
threads and `avail` includes them automatically when
their `requires` function evaluates true.

## what I did NOT do

- **Expose the dream as a modal or notification** —
  dreams are a quiet chronicle entry. Players who
  open the Chronicle panel find them; players who
  don't still have them happening in their realm's
  background. Matches the "no one speaks of them"
  framing.
- **Save-fixate the dream seed** — the flag
  (`dream_14`, `dream_28`, etc.) prevents re-firing,
  but the seed isn't persisted. If two separate
  saves of the same kingdom hit day 14, they get
  the same dream (good, deterministic), but if the
  player renames their kingdom mid-game it'd change
  future dream content (acceptable — that's a
  different kingdom now).
- **Hook music or sound** — a dream fragment could
  trigger a quiet chord. Out of scope; future tick
  could add if desired.
