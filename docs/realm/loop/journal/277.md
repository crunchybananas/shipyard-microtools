## why this surprises

This is the **FIRST INFERENCE-BY-ABSENCE
beat** in the corpus. All previous beats
about citizens (212 settler / 275 child;
240/245/247/249/252/253 named characters)
observe the citizen DIRECTLY — their
interiority, action, or worldview is
the focal subject.

277 inverts the observation mechanism:
the realm doesn't see the absent
citizen; it sees the **SHAPE** of their
absence — the empty seat at the inn,
unfilled for the third evening in a
row. Awareness mediated through
ROUTINE DISRUPTION rather than direct
encounter.

The detail "the third evening in a
row" is load-bearing:
- not first (could be coincidence, no
  signal yet)
- not every (would imply systematic
  surveillance)
- third-in-a-row — the threshold where
  habit-breakage becomes notable
  without becoming alarming

The implicit story (sick? dead? gone?
moved on like in 269 [code] departure
filing?) is left unsaid. The realm's
chronicle records ONLY the fact of
absence, not its cause.

276 promoted shape-axis pattern to

1. Does the prose surprise on its own

   "There is an afternoon").

All three: ship as shape-extension.

## meta-observation

This tick is a clean demonstration
of how a newly-promoted invariant
operates as authoring guidance:

1. Tick 276 promoted shape-axis to
   invariant.
2. Tick 277 had to ship a surprise.
3. The 276 decision tree provided
   the framing: a beat that fits
   existing register + introduces
   new angle ships as shape-
   extension.
4. The 277 prose was authored with
   that framing in mind from the
   start.

If 277 had been authored pre-276,
the framing question ("is this a new
sub-type? is it OUTSIDE?") would have
required deliberation each time. Post-
276 the framing is automatic; the
author's energy goes to PROSE QUALITY
not framing decisions.

This is the operational payoff of
invariant promotion — reducing
authoring friction by formalizing
defaults.
