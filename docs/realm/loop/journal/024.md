## hunt #1 — retroactive beats on old saves

**Setup**: simulate a pre-022 save by having
`G.buildings` contain a blacksmith + windmill but no
corresponding flags (`firstBlacksmith`, `firstWindmill`).
Call `checkStoryBeats()`.

**Result**: both beats fire. The player's chronicle now
shows "Hammer rings on anvil..." and "Sails turn above
the fields..." — timestamped at the *moment of load*,
not at the building's actual first-place time.

**Reading**: this is arguably desirable (existing saves
get the new narrative content) but creates timeline
confusion — a player who built a blacksmith on day 5,
then loads post-022 on day 50, sees a "first blade" beat
at day 50. Chronology is a lie.

**Severity**: low. Won't affect new games (fresh saves
won't have the gap). Only visible to players who had the
relevant buildings in a save from before 022 landed.

**Fix sketch**: in `checkStoryBeats()`, guard
retroactive firing by checking `G.day > 1`. Only fire the
beat if we're at or near Day 1. That way an old save
won't re-narrate. But then new players who build these
on day 10 wouldn't get the beat either. More correct:
check `Date.now()` against a setting that stored
"protocol version" when the save was created. Too much
complexity for a minor quirk. Suggest filing as a
`[code]` idea and moving on.

## hunt #2 — flag permanence

**Setup**: fire a chronicle beat for blacksmith, then
delete the blacksmith from `G.buildings`, then push a
new one. Call `checkStoryBeats()` again.

**Result**: flag `firstBlacksmith` stayed set, no re-fire.
`refiredBeats: 0`.

**Reading**: correct. "First" is forever. Even if the
player loses their first blacksmith to a raid and
rebuilds, the chronicle doesn't spam them with a
duplicate beat. Good.

## hunt #3 — zero-resource placement

**Setup**: zero out all resources (`G.resources.wood=0`
etc.), set `selectedBuild='farm'`, call
`placeBuilding('farm', 23, 22)`.

**Result**: returned `false`, building not added.

**Reading**: the `canAfford(type)` check at
`economy.js:60` correctly gates placement. Resources
aren't consumed on a refused placement.

## hunt #4 — undo + chronicle desynchronization (REAL BUG)

**Setup**:
1. Clear `firstBakery` flag, clear `G.chronicle`.
2. Place a bakery via `placeBuilding('bakery', 23, 22)`.
3. Call `checkStoryBeats()` — chronicle gains 1 entry
   ("First loaves cool..."), flag `firstBakery` is set.
4. Call `undoLastBuild()` — building removed.
5. Call `checkStoryBeats()` again.
6. Rebuild the bakery on a different tile.
7. Call `checkStoryBeats()`.

**Result**:

```
after place   → chronicle: 3, flag: true
after undo    → chronicle: 3, flag: true, buildings -1
after rebuild → chronicle: 3, flag: true  (no re-fire)
```

The chronicle retains the "First loaves cool..." entry
after the undo. The flag stays set. When the player
rebuilds later, no new beat fires. **The chronicle now
narrates an event that didn't persistently happen**, and
the actual first-build of the bakery gets no narrative
moment.

**Severity**: low-frequency, real. Undo is designed as a
"mistake correction" — a player who placed a bakery by
accident and undoes it shouldn't have "first loaves"
forever stamped in their story. But they do.

**Repro stability**: clean. 100%.

**Fix approaches** (not shipping this tick):

a. **Undo-aware flags**: stash `G.storyFlags` snapshot in
   `G._undoStack` entries; restore it on undo. Clean but
   requires touching the story system.
b. **Chronicle cleanup on undo**: also remove the most
   recent `milestone`-tagged chronicle entry. Messy
   because undo doesn't know which entries it caused.
c. **Don't flag until chronicle entry age > some
   threshold**: only lock the flag if 1+ tick has passed
   since the chronicle entry, so undo-within-the-same-
   tick gets reverted cleanly.
d. **Accept the edge case**: the player does something
   unusual (undo on a rare first-build) and the chronicle
   ends up slightly out-of-sync with reality. Some games
   live with worse.

Recommend (a) — it generalizes to ALL story flags, not
just first-build ones. Small scope per flag (a snapshot
map), clear semantics (undo restores the pre-place
state), plays well with the `undoLastBuild` interface
that already exists.
