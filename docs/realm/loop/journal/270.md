## why this surprises

This is the **FIRST MULTI-CHARACTER
BEAT IN THE CORPUS**. All previous
character-interiority beats (240/245/
247/249/252/253) feature ONE named
character ALONE in a moment of inner
life:
- 240 bard hidden art
- 245 smith unexplained departure
- 247 teacher small preservation
- 249 merchant meditative ritual
- 252 rival distant observation
- 253 mayor procedural witnessing

270 inverts the shape: **three named
characters CO-PRESENT** in the same
scene by chance. The realm has grown
socially dense enough that ordinary
co-presence becomes notable.

The lift line "the realm has grown
into the kind of place where this is
possible" captures the
social-density transition — not a
threshold of POPULATION (that's 230's
beat) but a threshold of NAMED-
CHARACTER coincidence. Three of six
named slots all at one place is not
high probability without a populated
inn culture supporting it.

266 precedents I considered three

   belongs INSIDE the character-
   interiority observation, not
   outside it.
3. **NEW SHAPE within the
   character-interiority observation**:
   ACCEPTED. The cast observation
   already had ONE shape (solitary-
   interiority, 6 uses). 270 adds
   a SECOND shape (confluence, 1
   use). Documented in narrative-
   surfaces.md AS a shape within the
   observation, NOT as a cluster
   sub-type.

This pattern (one observation can have
multiple shapes) is what 251's
canonical-role-matched register rule
already operates on — 6 character
interiorities, 6 different registers
matching 6 canonical roles. 270
extends the observation by adding
a SHAPE axis (solitary vs confluence)
orthogonal to the REGISTER axis
(per-character).
