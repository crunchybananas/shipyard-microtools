## the unforeseen effects

146 didn't predict these:

### 1. A paired-grammar emerged

148 (wanderer, day-crossing figure) + 152 (night-
shape, child-seen bright) turned out to be **more
than two beats** — they became a reusable pattern.
Rules crystallized: misc tag, static string, no
variants, no "name" word, elder-silence register,
timing-gate for plausibility. 155 (the-archivist)
documented the grammar as an invariant in
narrative-surfaces.md. 156 (refactor-only) added a
29-line template comment at the authoring site.

The realm now has a **formalized pattern** for
giving ambient entities (owls, frogs, rams, trade-
ships, hawks, etc) their first narrative surface
without growing the founder arc. This is a
durable payoff — it outlasts the moratorium and
shapes future ticks.

**The moratorium FORCED the pattern into
existence.** Without the constraint, 148 might
have been a one-off; with it, 152 was compelled to
re-use the shape, which crystallized the grammar.

### 2. Schema evolution happened mid-moratorium

144 (the-fixer, pre-moratorium but in the same
window) extended NARRATIVE_BEATS schema with
`after: (G) => void` callback. The 3-or-more-need
rule held (onFire had 3+ callers at 123; `after`
had 1 at 144, justified by legitimate side effect).
Moratorium didn't cause this — but the archivist
tick at 155 documented it alongside the ambient-
entity grammar. **Two schema-level capabilities
captured in one doc update.**

### 3. The audio skeptic flag landed

154 (the-skeptic) flagged a sibling over-
investment: 9 audio cues shipped across 18 audio-
axis ticks (106-149) with 0 live-auditions. The
moratorium made it easier to schedule a review-
type tick instead of a composer, and the review
exposed a pattern-problem that might otherwise
have accumulated more audio cues before being
noticed. **The moratorium created schedule room
for meta-analysis.**

### 4. Founder arc didn't suffer

No realm-reading test was run, but the 7/5/4 count
held across the moratorium. The founder arc is
**quantitatively unchanged** — founder1 still has
7 surfaces, founder2 has 5, founder3 has 4 —
while 4 NEW beats (142 distant-letter, 147 great-
storm, 148 wanderer, 152 night-shape) added
**non-founder** narrative density. The realm's
narrative FABRIC grew; the founder THREADS didn't.

## did the moratorium serve the arc?

**Yes, clearly.**

Evidence:
- 3/≥3 target HIT (minimum)
- 0 founder-surface increase (well under the ≤ 1
  limit)
- Durable pattern created (ambient-entity grammar)
- Schedule room for meta-analysis (154 skeptic)
- Zero narrative-quality regression detected

Counterfactual: without the moratorium, 146's
analysis suggests ticks 147-156 would likely have
produced 2-3 MORE founder surfaces (116/128/134/
138 were the recent pattern — ~1 every 5-6 ticks).
At 16 + 3 = 19 founder surfaces, the arc would
have crossed a saturation threshold (100's letter
already flagged founder1 at 7 as "concern").

**The contrarian argument at 146 was correct.**
146 predicted: rebalancing was a crutch; 10 ticks
of discipline would reveal non-founder narrative
capacity. 147-156 delivered exactly that — 4 new
non-founder beats + a reusable grammar.

## policy going forward

### 1. Moratorium formally CLOSES.

From 158 onward, new founder surfaces are
permitted. But the ambient-entity grammar is now
a **preferred alternative** for acknowledgment-
style beats. If a beat could reasonably be an
ambient-entity acknowledgment instead of a
founder reference, prefer the ambient pattern.

### 2. Founder-surface budget.

Soft cap: founder arc at 16 surfaces (7/5/4) is
**probably enough**. Additional founder surfaces
should:
- Close a long-open filed idea (re-shipper
  justification)
- Resolve a structural gap (like 103's end-of-
  realm requiem)
- NOT just "add flavor"

If a surprise tick leans founder-focal by default,
flip to ambient/location/weather/object instead.

### 3. The 5-tick surprise mandate.

MANIFEST Evolution mandate already says "at least
1 in 5 ticks should be surprise OR propose
something not in ideas.md." The moratorium
effectively elevated this to "1 in 5 surprise,
and biased toward non-founder." **Keep the bias
explicitly** even though the formal moratorium
ended.

### 4. Next the-contrarian target.

146 was 135 ticks overdue at the time of firing.
Next the-contrarian could audit other arcs that
have grown quietly:
- Audio axis (154 already flagged)
- Named-character mechanics (3/6 graduated;
  154's skeptic notes cumulative-budget concern)
- CSS type-scale migration (140/143/150 = 3 ticks
  so far; is it worth the ongoing maintenance?)
- Dream pool (039/054/055/097 extended; current
  size?)

### 5. Milestone-check-in protocol.

146 + 157 established a useful pattern:
**propose constraint + pre-schedule check-in
ticks**. Future contrarian/skeptic ticks that
propose multi-tick policies should set an
explicit check-in tick. Makes proposals falsifiable
and prevents drift.
