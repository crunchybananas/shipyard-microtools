# 023 — the-minimalist

**Challenge:** the-minimalist — accepted. First use of
this persona. 020 recommended it twice (milestone letter
+ next-tick); 022 recommended it again. After 22 ticks
of mostly-additive work (9 `[code]` ticks, all shipped

removing one thing.

## the ground rule

The-minimalist challenge: *"If you could remove one
thing from realm to make it better, what and why? Argue
it."*

Ground rule I'm holding: the pick has to be something
where *absence* is better than *presence*. Not "this
could be better" — "this is making things worse just by
existing." If I can't argue that, I'm not being
minimalist; I'm being editorial.

## candidates I considered (and rejected)

- **An ambient enhancement** (pollen bursts / dragonflies
  / tower banners / etc.). 80+ ambient effects were added
  by the old-protocol loops. Some are probably unnoticed.
  But rejecting on principle: "unnoticed" ≠ "worse by
  existing." An unseen flourish costs nothing and
  occasionally rewards attentive play. The-minimalist
  isn't "cut dead weight" — that's the-skeptic's job.
- **The Skip-tutorial button on step 1.** 002 flagged it
  as "defensive." I considered arguing removal — force
  the player through 1 step before offering the escape.
  Rejected: the skip button is an act of respect. A
  returning player shouldn't be trapped in a tutorial
  they've already completed. Respect is not friction.
- **The Research system.** 8 techs gating 20+ buildings.
  Tempting to argue for removal on novice-friction
  grounds. Rejected: research is the core progression
  loop. Removing it would collapse the game into
  flat-difficulty.
- **The 5-scenario structure.** Novice can't tell them
  apart on first glance (008 fixed that with descriptions).
  Rejected: 5 scenarios give the game 5× the replay
  surface. Any consolidation would be a losing trade.
- **The `Welcome to Realm` toast on game start.** 006
  already moved it out of the viewport center. Could
  argue for removing it entirely now (the tutorial tip
  already signals "here's what to do"). Rejected: the
  toast is a "the game has started" confirmation beat
  that serves first-few-seconds UX. It's redundant with
  the tutorial, sure, but the redundancy is the point —
  two signals that *something happened*.

## what I'm arguing to remove

**The second objective of the Peaceful Valley scenario:
"Research stonework, then build a Church."**

`js/scenarios.js:14` defines it:

```js
{ text: 'Research stonework, then build a Church', check: () => G.buildings.some(b => b.type === 'church') },
```

This is the only objective in all 5 scenarios that:

1. **Chains two actions in one text.** Every other
   objective is atomic: *"Train 10 soldiers"*,
   *"Build a Castle"*, *"Reach 30 population"*,
   *"Accumulate 500 gold"*, *"Build 2 Iron Mines"*.
   This one uniquely says "do A, then do B."
2. **Names a specific tech explicitly in the objective
   text.** No other objective mentions research by
   name. The player has to know that "stonework" is a
   tech to parse the objective at all.
3. **Has a check function that ignores half its own
   text.** The `check` is only
   `G.buildings.some(b => b.type === 'church')`. The
   "research stonework" half isn't actually verified —
   building a church is the only real test. The text
   is lying about the requirement.
4. **Is actually implicit in the next tech hop.**
   Building a church requires masonry (verified in
   tech.js `unlocks: ['quarry', 'wall', 'church']`).
   A player who places a church has by definition
   already researched masonry (technically "masonry"
   not "stonework" — the objective text uses a name
   the game doesn't use elsewhere).

Five problems in one line.

## what happens when it's removed

The Peaceful Valley scenario becomes:

```
- Reach 10 population
- Build a Church         (simplified from "Research stonework, then build a Church")
- Reach 80% happiness
```

What's lost:

- **An explicit pointer toward research** for novices
  who otherwise might not notice the Research button.
  Non-zero loss; the novice finding from 007 + 010 was
  that research is under-signposted.

What's gained:

- Text that matches the game's terminology (no
  "stonework" renaming of "masonry").
- Check-function that matches its own text.
- Atomic objectives throughout all 5 scenarios.
- A staircase toward research that the *tutorial*
  handles (step 6: "Click Research in the top bar to
  unlock new buildings like Quarry, Market, and
  more!") — putting the research-push in the tutorial
  where it belongs, not the mission panel.

Net: the mission panel gets more consistent. The
research nudge moves to where it's already doing work.
The lying-check becomes truthful.

## concrete change (not shipping this tick)

`scenarios.js:14`:

```diff
- { text: 'Research stonework, then build a Church', check: () => G.buildings.some(b => b.type === 'church') },
+ { text: 'Build a Church', check: () => G.buildings.some(b => b.type === 'church') },
```

One string edit. The check function already passes as-is.

This is 15 characters of removal (the *"Research
stonework, then "* prefix). The minimalist claim:
**removing those 15 characters makes realm measurably
better** along four axes (terminology consistency,
truthful checks, atomic objectives, cleaner mission
panel).

## what I did NOT do

- **Ship the change.** This is `[review]`, per
  MANIFEST. A future `[code]` tick can pick up this
  prescription. The journal *is* the deliverable.
- **Argue to remove more than one thing.** The
  challenge is specifically "one thing." The
  Peaceful-Church objective is my single strongest pick.
  A broader minimalist pass would pick differently —
  maybe multiple chained tutorial steps, or the
  Industrial scenario's conjunction in "Build a Windmill
  + Bakery" (same chained-objective problem, deferred to
  that scenario's own argument).
- **Actually survey every enhancement.** My dismissal of
  "cut an ambient enhancement" was principled (unseen ≠
  worse) rather than empirical. A later `the-skeptic`
  tick could go through enhancements.js and argue-to-cut
  with evidence.
