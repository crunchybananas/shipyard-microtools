## why this shape

- **Defensive-industrial family palette per 181
  review.** Stone color `[0.36, 0.32, 0.30]`
  matches tower 170's pre-loop tone but warmer
  toward iron-grey, distinguishing the smithy
  from purely-defensive structures. Same family
  but distinct subtype.
- **Walls TALLER than tavern (1.00 vs 0.85
  height).** Industrial buildings have higher
  ceilings (forges need ventilation; canvas
  drawBlacksmith wall is 22 tall vs 18 for
  tavern). 3D mesh inherits that proportion.
- **Flat parapet vs tavern's pitched roof.**
  Industrial roofline; matches barracks 183's
  flat-parapet-with-crenellations choice (sans
  crenellations because canvas drawBlacksmith
  doesn't have them — defensive-industrial
  family but military vs trade variants).
- **Heavy chimney projects above parapet.** 6
  units wide × 14 tall × 6 deep. Visible iso-
  silhouette element distinguishing from
  tower/barracks at iso angle.
- **Forge-fire window as bright cube.** The 3D
  prototype's per-vertex color is the only
  color mechanism (no emissive shading, no
  glow shader). A small saturated-amber cube
  `[0.98, 0.65, 0.18]` against dark walls
  reads as "glowing arched window" at iso
  scale. Acceptable affordance without
  needing emissive material.
- **Anvil prop in 3 boxes.** Base block + body
  + horn extending forward. Captures the
  silhouette without complex geometry.
- **Reused pushBox only.** No new primitive.
