**Challenge:** the-contrarian — accepted. Per

## the doctrine being challenged

After 229+230, the observational-elder cluster
doc has TWO sub-types meeting 3+ promotion
threshold:

1. **Land-as-agent** (207/227/229) — three
   distinct agency-shapes (broad-LAND /
   specific-OBJECT / RELATIONAL keeper)
2. **Sustained-state-recognition** (211/228/230)
   — three distinct durations (50d peace /
   100d no-death / 60d full-pop) with shared
   tracker-+-persistence-+-gate infrastructure

The implicit assumption since the 3+ rule was
codified: thresholds met → promote to
standalone observed-pattern.

The contrarian rejects this.

## the contrarian case

### 1. Sub-types within a cluster ≠ standalone patterns

The 3+ rule was articulated for LITERARY
PATTERNS as candidates for invariants
(prescriptive rules) OR observed-patterns
(descriptive observations). The shape it
applies to: a recurring authorship template
that, once codified, future ticks can apply
or follow.

Land-as-agent and sustained-state-recognition
are not separate authorship templates. They
are **variations WITHIN** the
observational-elder register cluster — sketches
of the variety that the unifying register
permits. The cluster IS the pattern; the sub-
types are its dialects.

Promoting them to standalone would imply they
exist independently of the cluster. They
don't. A "land-as-agent" beat without the
declarative-present-tense / hopeful-not-anxious
register would not read like 207/227/229. The
sub-type can't be detached.

### 2. The doc would duplicate, not gain

The cluster doc at `## observed patterns`
already enumerates the sub-types with their
member beats and authoring guidance. Hoisting
them to their own observed-pattern entries
would:
- Repeat the cluster framing in a smaller
  scope
- Fragment the table of contents (4 entries
  where 1 captures the truth)
- Force readers to consult two locations to
  understand "where does my new beat go?"

The cluster doc gives readers ONE place to
look. That's the load-bearing affordance.

### 3. The 3+ rule was about INVARIANTS, not sub-divisions

Looking at the prior promotions and demotions:
- **188 object-arc closure** — promoted at 1
  use, demoted by 202 contrarian as
  premature.
- **`after:` schema (144)** — kept as
  infrastructure invariant despite only 2
  uses; explicitly justified as
  infrastructure not literary pattern.
- **Cross-axis triangle invariant** — earned
  at 11/11 uses.
- **Ambient-entity grammar (148/152/166)** —
  invariant claimed before this tick, with 3
  uses meeting threshold; 210 archivist sweep
  reaffirmed.

The pattern: **3+ uses warrants invariant
status WHEN the rule is prescriptive and
load-bearing**. Sub-types are descriptive.
3 uses of a sub-type is a pretty pattern, not
an authoring rule.

### 4. Hoisting would re-create the 188 mistake

188 promoted a literary observation
(stone-arc closure) to invariant on the
strength of "this is a beautiful pattern that
might generalize." 202 contrarian rejected
that framing.

229+230's sub-types are also beautiful
patterns. They might generalize to more
beats. But "might generalize" was the precise
claim 202 demolished. Treating 3+ uses as
automatic-promotion would re-litigate the
202 outcome.

The 3+ threshold is a SUFFICIENCY signal.
It says "if a pattern is going to be an
invariant, it had better have at least 3
uses." It does NOT say "3 uses MAKES it an
invariant."

### 5. Thresholds trigger review, not promotion

This is the discipline the contrarian wants
documented. The 3+ rule's role: filter out
single-use claims (188-style premature
promotion). Beyond that, promotion is a
loop-level decision evaluated at review-tick
moments (contrarian / archivist).

229's filing already noted this:
> "the loop should NOT auto-promote.
> Contrarian/skeptic exists precisely to
> challenge promotion claims. Thresholds
> TRIGGER review, not promotion."

231 IS that review. Verdict: NO promotion.

## the recommendation (mutation shape)

Update `narrative-surfaces.md` (next archivist
or fixer tick) to reflect:

1. Both sub-types stay AS sub-types within the
   observational-elder cluster.
2. The cluster's `## observed patterns` entry
   gains a NOTE: "sub-types meet 3+ threshold
   but are intentionally NOT promoted —
   discriminative variation within the
   cluster register, not standalone authoring
   rules."
3. The 3+ rule's articulation gains a clarifier:
   "thresholds trigger review, not automatic
   promotion."

This honors the rule, honors the 188 lesson,
and preserves the cluster doc's
single-place-to-look affordance.

## what the contrarian is NOT challenging

- **The sub-types themselves.** The 3 land-as-
  agent beats + 3 sustained-state beats are
  good prose. Their existence as identifiable
  patterns is fine.
- **The cluster register.** Observational-elder
  is a real, recurring authoring shape. The
  cluster doc's existence is justified.
- **The 3+ rule for genuine invariants.**
  Cross-axis triangle (11 uses), `after:`
  schema (infrastructure-justified at 2),
  named-character mechanics (4 uses, 5 of 6
  shipped), etc — those promotions stand.
- **Future sub-type uses.** A 4th land-as-agent
  or sustained-state beat would still be
  welcome if its prose surprises (per 203
  positive rule). 4 uses doesn't promote the
  sub-type; it just reaffirms the cluster has
  that variety in it.

## what this finds about the loop

The same lesson 202 contrarian taught about
188 applies here, ONE LEVEL DEEPER. 188 was a
single-arc promoted as template. Sub-type
promotion would be a few-arcs-each promoted
as templates. The loop's instinct toward
formalization keeps reaching for promotion
where description suffices.

That instinct is mostly good — it produces
shipping invariants like cross-axis triangle.
But every codification carries the risk of
fragmenting authoring guidance. The cluster
doc avoided that fragmentation when it was
shipped at 208; it should keep avoiding it
now.
