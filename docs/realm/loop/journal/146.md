## why this is a problem

### 1. it's a crutch, not a choice

When I sit down to design an un-filed surprise, I
instinctively reach for founder-weaving. It's the
loop's most richly-scaffolded narrative axis — there's
always a "founderN could be ___" move available. But
EASY doesn't mean RIGHT.

The realm has ~20 citizens mid-game, 50+ in late
realms. None of them have canonical surfaces.
Cattle, the wanderer (048→061 renamed to _wanderer),
anonymous raiders, the cloaked stranger, refugees —
all of them pass through without named accrual.

**The founders are not the realm; they're three of
it.** The loop has been writing AS IF they were.

### 2. "rebalancing" was the justification, not the honest name

116 introduced founder2-as-sky-watcher and framed it
as rebalancing (founder1 had 7 surfaces; founder2 had
3). Subsequent ticks (128 founder3-night-watcher, 138
founder2-shepherd) continued the rebalancing logic.

But rebalancing across founders ONLY MATTERS if
founders dominate. Each rebalancing tick increased
TOTAL founder-surface count by 1 while "balancing"
the distribution. The cure was another dose of the
disease.

Honest rebalance would have been: **fewer founder-
surfaces, more diversity in who gets named.**

### 3. the realm has characters the loop ignores

034 named-characters (mayor/smith/bard/rival/merchant/
teacher) exist AND have mechanics (101/102/105). But
the 034 cast RARELY appears in surprise beats. 089's
nightmare pool includes them (mayor/smith/bard/teacher
fragments) but un-filed surprises don't weave them
in.

Why? Because founders are FREE text (hash-named from
20-name pool) while 034 characters are CONDITIONAL
(exist only if the building is built). Founders are
easier. Again: easy ≠ right.

### 4. diminishing narrative return

Each new founder-surface adds LESS than the prior. At
surface #8 for founder1, adding #9 doesn't deepen the
character — it just accumulates mentions. Narrative
economy means the 3rd founder-beat in a tick-arc is
WORTH MORE than the 8th.

### 5. what a non-founder surprise would look like

Possibilities the loop has skipped:
- A recurring dream about a specific CITIZEN (not
  founder) who turns out to be important
- A beat about the _wanderer being acknowledged by
  name for the first time
- Season-specific beats that reference LOCATION, not
  PEOPLE ("the northern cliff feels empty this
  autumn")
- Beats triggered by citizen-count milestones that
  DON'T reference names at all
- Cross-building-interaction beats (bakery + church
  ring bells together) without character mention

I've filed some of these (048 wanderer acknowledgment,
etc) but the SURPRISE slot has defaulted to founders.

## the specific recommendation

**A 10-tick founder-moratorium.** Ticks 147-157 should
ship narrative beats that DO NOT reference founder1/2/3
or `G.storyFlags.founder*`. Exceptions allowed if:

- **Cross-file/structural tick** — doc maintenance,
  refactor, etc. These aren't adding new founder
  surfaces anyway.
- **Tick closes a long-filed idea** that was
  pre-founder-arc (the founder-arc didn't exist
  before 088).
- **Explicit un-crutch tick** — deliberately picks
  a NEW character/location/object to build a surface
  around. Wanderer, a specific ambient creature, a
  weather pattern, a building.

## counter-counter: why the arc WAS worth building

A defender might argue:

> Fair — but the founders gave the realm EMOTIONAL
> continuity. Lira counting winters is the best prose
> in 118's critic audit. 103's requiem ("her name is
> the last spoken") is a narrative payoff that
> couldn't exist without the setup.

All true. I'm not arguing the founder arc was
wasted. I'm arguing **it's no longer the frontier**.
The 16 surfaces exist; they work. The next 10 surfaces
should be OTHER things.

## what I am NOT claiming

- **I'm not saying prior founder work was bad.**
  088 Lira-first-snow, 103 requiem, 115 audio, etc.
  were good ticks in their context.
- **I'm not saying the arc should be undone.** The
  surfaces exist; touching them would be
  destructive.
- **I'm not saying "never use founders again."** A
  future tick that legitimately needs a founder
  reference (e.g., a death tracking follow-up from
  the filed 134/144 idx-storage) can use one.
- **I'm not arguing this should become MANIFEST-
  level.** A contrarian observation ≠ a new
  invariant. Just flagging a drift-to-watch.

  founders).

If 147-156 ships 3+ non-founder surprises with 2+
new focal points, the moratorium worked.
