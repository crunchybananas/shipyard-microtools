## why this shape

- **095's actual finding.** The granary winter
  cap floated above the dome as a wider ellipse
  (w=15 vs dome radius 11). 094 set it; 096
  adjusted y=-26→-22 and h=4→3 (narrowed the gap)
  but didn't fix the SHAPE mismatch — ellipse
  sitting above a dome still reads as "two
  stacked domes." 158 makes the snow an arc
  matching the dome's curve.
- **Why this closes the "dome-conforming" ask
  precisely.** 095-filed idea: "cap CONFORMS to
  dome curve, not ellipse above." Literal: the
  snow arc now shares the dome's center and
  radius; it IS part of the dome's surface
  geometry.
- **Why angle range π+0.55 to -0.55.** Sin(0.55)
  ≈ 0.52; cos(0.55) ≈ 0.85. So the left edge of
  the cap lands at `(x - 9.4, y - 15.8)` and the
  right edge at `(x + 9.4, y - 15.8)`. The cap
  spans ~18.8 px wide (slightly less than the
  old 15 half-width ellipse but perceptually
  similar) and the apex at `(x, y - 21)` sits
  exactly where the dome's finial already lives.
  Hence the snow LOOKS like it sits atop the
  dome rather than floating above it.
- **Icicles on-dome.** Prior icicles sat on the
  ellipse's bottom edge (`snowY + 1`); the new
  set sits on the dome's actual curve. Icicles
  near the apex are higher (y-21) and icicles
  toward the edges lower (y-16) — matches
  physical snow melt/drip shape.
- **Table entry removal, not retained as a
  fallback.** The dome-conforming branch is
  always-on for granary; no condition where
  `_WINTER_CAPS.granary` would be read. Keeping
  it as dead config would confuse future
  readers. Breadcrumb comment preserves the
  history.
- **No table-schema change.** Only granary has
  a dome shape; other buildings have flat /
  peaked / complex rooflines that the ellipse
  cap already handles well. Generalizing the
  conforming logic would be premature (the 3+
  rule: no other candidate building has this
  dome-conforming need yet).
