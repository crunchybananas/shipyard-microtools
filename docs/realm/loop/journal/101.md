## the change

`docs/realm/js/tech.js:updateResearch` — 2 lines added:

```js
const teacherBonus = G.namedCharacters?.teacher ? 0.1 : 0;
const speedMult = 1 + schools * 0.5 + teacherBonus;
```

Teacher bonus is **additive** on top of the existing school
multiplier. A realm with 2 active schools + teacher:
- Pre-101: `1 + 2×0.5 = 2.0×`
- Post-101: `1 + 2×0.5 + 0.1 = 2.1×`

A realm with teacher only (no active school):
- Pre-101: `1.0×` (baseline research)
- Post-101: `1.1×`

A realm with no teacher:
- Pre-101 = Post-101: identical. No bonus without naming.

## why this shape

- **Additive +10%, not multiplicative.** Multiplicative on the
  school bonus would compound: 2 schools × 1.1 = 2.2× vs the
  current additive 2.1×. Additive is more predictable and
  makes the teacher bonus *a constant contribution* rather
  than a "scales with scale" modifier. 10% feels right — big
  enough to notice in a long research, small enough to feel
  flavorful rather than mandatory.
- **No school-present check.** 034's `ensureTeacher` fires
  when first school is placed, so teacher presence implies
  school has existed. If the player later destroys the
  school, teacher persists in the realm's named-character
  registry — and the bonus persists. Narrative interpretation:
  "the teacher's knowledge persists even if the schoolhouse
  is ash."
- **No chronicle beat.** 034's character-intro beat already
  announces teacher's arrival. Adding "your research is now
  10% faster" would be a mechanic-tutorial moment; too
  explicit. Mechanics without UI ceremony reward attentive
  players.
- **No UI indicator.** Matches the 048/058 "ambient
  silent" philosophy. A player reading the research-progress
  bar won't see "+10% TEACHER BONUS" floating above — they'll
  just notice, over long play, that research completes
  slightly faster than expected. Research rate already has
  school bonuses silently applied; teacher fits the pattern.
- **Graduates the system without shifting the balance much.**
  A +10% bonus on a ~10 research techs × 100 days per tech
  = small time savings across a realm. Doesn't break
  scenario balance. Doesn't make teacher mandatory. First
  step into "named characters affect gameplay."

## what this does NOT do

- **Give other characters mechanics.** Merchant could
  influence trade prices (050 suggested). Smith could
  affect combat. Bard could influence happiness. Mayor
  could unlock scenarios. All filed as follow-ups. 101
  picked the minimum-risk highest-payoff one.
- **Make the bonus visible.** No UI indicator, no
  research-panel hint. Filed as optional follow-up.
- **Balance-tune the bonus.** +10% is a guess; a future
  play tick (the-completionist or the-speedrunner) could
  measure whether 10% is too low/high.
- **Add a "teacher's daughter becomes next teacher" or
  generation mechanic.** That's entire-game scope.
- **Destroy the bonus if school burns.** Teacher persists
  in named-character state; bonus persists. Narrative
  choice, not a bug.
- **Update narrative-surfaces.md.** No new chronicle
  surface; pure game mechanic. Doc unchanged.
