## findings

### HIGH — peak-roof + chimney family blurs (3-way)

`drawHouse` (render.js:3080), `drawTavern`
(render.js:4144), and `drawBakery` (render.js:5178)
all produce the same core silhouette: a rectangular
wall box (~20 wide, ~16 tall) + a triangular peaked
roof centered at `s.y - 28 to −30` + a vertical
chimney stick.

Rough outline comparison:

```
HOUSE          TAVERN         BAKERY
  .|.            . .            .|.
 .| |.          ./.\.          ./ \.
 /   \          /   \          /   \
/_ _ _\        /_ _ _\        /_ _ _\
|  o  |        |sign |        |  o  |
|_____|        |_____|        |_____|
```

- House chimney: `ctx.fillRect(chimneyX, s.y-35, 4, 8)`
  — 4px wide, at y-35 (render.js:3164)
- Bakery chimney: `ctx.fillRect(s.x+3, s.y-38, 5, 14)`
  — 5px wide, at y-38 (render.js:~5190)
- Tavern: has a sign-board attached to the front wall
  (not above the roof line in silhouette)

The differentiators between the three are all *inside
the silhouette* or *along one edge*, not topology
differences. A pure black-silhouette rendering would
make house≈bakery≈tavern essentially
indistinguishable at default zoom. The bakery chimney
is slightly chunkier (5 vs 4 and +3px taller) but the
edge distance is within 2-3 pixels of the house's —
negligible against a 20×28 overall bounding box.

**Why this matters:** in a working settlement at
normal zoom, these are the three buildings that
appear most often in the same visual frame (all
residential/commercial near the center of town).
Players trying to find "my tavern" in a cluster of
buildings must rely on color (awnings, sign boards)
rather than shape. Strip the color (dusk, far zoom,
accessibility modes, minimap) and the cluster becomes
a wall of identical peaked roofs.

### MEDIUM — barracks ≈ blacksmith (wide stone block)

`drawBarracks` (render.js:3763) and `drawBlacksmith`
(render.js:5510) both use:

- front-wall rect ~23-25 wide × 20 tall, starting at
  `s.y - 22 to -24`
- isometric right-face extension (+4 to +5 px)
- horizontal/vertical stone-block mortar texture

Primitive dimensions:
- Barracks: `fillRect(s.x-13, s.y-24, 25, 20)` +
  right face to x+17
- Blacksmith: `fillRect(s.x-12, s.y-24, 23, 20)` +
  right face to x+16

**Delta: 2 pixels of width.** The forge glow
(`drawBlacksmith` has a warm orange window) is a
color signal, not a silhouette one. Barracks has
crenellation variants at the top edge (in some
variants), blacksmith is flat. But both are squat
dark stone blocks at identical heights.

The only reliable silhouette differentiator is the
barracks' optional crenellation. That's a weak
signal.

### LOW — farm ≈ cowpen (ground-level)

Both render at/near ground level:

- Farm: iso rhombus field + scarecrow stick at y-14
  (render.js:3170, 3312)
- Cowpen: rectangular fence at y-18 + low rails
  (render.js:5275)

Under strong silhouette-only conditions the thin
scarecrow stick (1-2 px wide) and thin fence posts
(2 px wide each) might all read as "a few thin
verticals on a ground patch." But they differ
enough — cowpen has 4 posts in a rectangular
pattern, farm has 1 center scarecrow — that side-
by-side comparison resolves them. LOW priority.

## recommended silhouette tweak (pick ONE)

The cleanest single-line improvement for this tick's
finding: **raise the tavern's sign board so it
projects above the roof line**.

Right now `drawTavern` renders the sign attached to
the front wall (below the roof peak at y-30).
Lifting the sign to project 4-6 pixels ABOVE the
roof — as a cantilevered pole-and-board element
perpendicular to the facade — would:

1. Break tavern out of the house/bakery family
   immediately (silhouette now has an extra
   horizontal bar sticking above the peak)
2. Reinforce the medieval-tavern visual language
   (taverns historically had projecting signs)
3. Cost ~6 lines of `fillRect` + `strokeStyle`

Fix shape:

```js
// At end of drawTavern, add projecting sign pole:
ctx.fillStyle = '#6a4e2a'; // pole
ctx.fillRect(s.x + 8, s.y - 34, 2, 6);
ctx.fillStyle = boardColor;
ctx.fillRect(s.x + 6, s.y - 40, 10, 6);
// subtle shadow line under sign
ctx.strokeStyle = 'rgba(0,0,0,0.4)';
ctx.strokeRect(s.x + 6, s.y - 40, 10, 6);
```

Alternative recommendation (bigger): **give bakery
a domed oven bulge** protruding from one side at
base-level. Silhouette would gain a clear "lump"
that neither house nor tavern has. ~10 lines.

## what I tried (and abandoned)

Spent ~30 minutes building a canvas-pixel silhouette
sampler:

1. Spawn one building at known world tile, force
   camera to center on it, render
2. Crop a bounding box around the building
3. Classify each pixel as "grass" / "silhouette"
4. Produce an ASCII mask + per-column skyline profile

Failed because:
- Starting tile (40,35 or 40,40) was not reliably
  grass in the generated world — often water or
  beach → 100% "non-grass" classification → entire
  bbox flagged as silhouette.
- Moving the camera to a known grass area without
  an accessible `findGrassTile()` helper meant
  guessing at the generated world's layout.
- At dawn/dusk the night tint lowers grass
  brightness below my grass threshold, breaking
  the classifier.

Filed as a 046 [code] follow-up: expose a
`G.debug.renderBuildingIsolated(type)` helper that
draws a single building onto an offscreen canvas
against a known background. That would make this
challenge trivially automatable for future ticks.
