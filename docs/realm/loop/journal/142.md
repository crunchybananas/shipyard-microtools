## why this shape

- **10 distant-kingdom names × 6 news fragments = 60
  unique realm/news pairs.** Deterministic per
  kingdom via two separate hash keys (`_distant_name`
  and `_distant_news`), so every Avalon hears from
  the same distant place with the same news. Shared
  dream across re-plays.
- **"Un-realm" narrative surface.** Every beat prior
  has been about THIS realm — its people, stone,
  constellation, winters. 142 is the first beat that
  references a place NOT the realm. Implies a wider
  world.
- **Gate: `firstMarket` + day ≥ 60.** A merchant
  network to CARRY the letter + enough time to have
  established trade. Early realms without markets
  (peaceful-start without scenarios that push trade)
  may never see this beat — which is thematically
  right.
- **Tag: `event` (reuse per 075 invariant).** The
  cloaked-stranger / bard-arrival / rival-tribute
  beats already use `event`. A distant letter is
  another kind of visitor-news event. No new tag
  needed.
- **Echo-eligible.** `event` is in `_ECHO_SOURCE_
  TAGS` (063). A 059 echo could resurface this beat:
  "Old folk remember the traveler from Norrith."
  Nice compound.
- **News lines are DELIBERATELY UNCANNY.** "A child
  born who speaks only in rhymes," "the rivers run
  backward one morning" — other realms are
  weirder-than-ours. Hints at narrative richness
  beyond the island without describing it.
- **Medieval-folkloric names** — Norrith, Velar,
  Blackwell, Sylvain. Consistent with the realm's
  voice (no classical-Greek, no modern; all would
  fit in an Old English / Anglo-Scottish map).
- **No founder reference.** 142 deliberately steps
  OUT of the founder-arc. The realm is established
  enough (day 60+, merchant network) to look beyond
  itself. Intentional variety from 116/122/128/134/
  138 which were all founder-centric.
