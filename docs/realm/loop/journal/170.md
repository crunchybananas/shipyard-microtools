## what tower.svg expresses that canvas drawTower doesn't have

- **Cross-shaped arrow slits.** Canvas has plain
  vertical rectangles for slits (2 lines:
  `fillRect(s.x-1, s.y-28, 2, 5)`). SVG ships the
  historically authentic CRUCIFORM slit (vertical
  slit with small horizontal cross-arms at
  midpoint) — the actual medieval defensive shape.
- **Iron lantern bracket.** Canvas tower has no
  lantern. SVG adds a small iron bracket extending
  from the side wall with a caged lantern + warm
  glow inside (radial gradient yellow-orange).
  Adds a "this tower is occupied / watched"
  signal.
- **Banner pennant on flag pole.** Canvas tower
  has no flag (only the central castle does in the
  canvas roster). SVG tower gets a red triangular
  pennant on a small pole atop the parapet —
  **doesn't compete with castle's gold flag**, but
  marks the tower as garrisoned.
- **Moss at base.** Canvas tower is pristine. SVG
  has subtle green-brown moss patches at the
  doorway corners + shadow band — organic decay
  detail that ages the structure.
- **Stone-block pattern.** Canvas does horizontal
  mortar lines via `for` loop. SVG declares
  `<pattern id="towerBlocks">` (matching the
  castle/church/windmill family) and applies via
  `fill="url(#towerBlocks)"`.
- **Crenellation highlights.** Canvas merlons are
  flat fillRect shapes. SVG adds per-merlon top-
  edge sunlit stroke highlight, reading more
  three-dimensional.
- **Iron studs on door.** Canvas door has plain
  arched dark fill. SVG ships hinge bands + 2
  visible studs + ring-style handle.
