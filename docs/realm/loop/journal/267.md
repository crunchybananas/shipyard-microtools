## why this shape

- **Spawn from `after:` callback (per
  144 schema), not direct trigger.**
  144's after-field is the canonical
  hook for chronicle-adjacent side
  effects. Mirrors how 134 namesake
  uses it for citizen rename. Keeps
  the side effect attached to the
  beat declaration — readers of
  story.js see the visual and textual
  components together.
- **Screen-space `offsetX`
  accumulator.** Tile-space movement
  would couple the streak to camera
  motion (player panning would shift
  the star erratically). Screen-space
  via offsetX makes the streak's
  motion independent of camera —
  natural-feeling sweep regardless of
  what the player is doing.
- **3-layer rendering (trail / halo /
  core).** Single bright dot would
  read as a UI artifact or stuck
  pixel. The fading trail unambiguously
  signals MOTION, the halo softens
  the edge so it reads as light, the
  core anchors the head. Mirrors how
  postfx (lines 41-66) renders bloom
  on bright pixels — same authoring
  language.
- **Color #fff8dc (cornsilk) for
  trail, pure white for core.** Same
  palette as the existing 'spark'
  particle (render.js:2680). Reads
  as STAR, not torch (which uses
  oranges).
- **Lifetime ~1.4s.** Long enough to
  catch the eye if the player is
  looking at the screen; short enough
  that a chronicle entry written
  alongside is the durable record.
  The text is the chronicle; the
  visual is ephemeral — matching the
  beat's lift line "the star is gone
  before anyone can ask if anyone
  else saw it."
