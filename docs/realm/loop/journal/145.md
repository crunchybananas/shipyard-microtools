## the build

### 1. `docs/realm/js/audio.js` — new `shepherds-song` case

```js
case 'shepherds-song': {
  makeOsc(ctx, dest, 'sine', 392, 0.045, 0.15, 2.5, t);
  makeOsc(ctx, dest, 'sine', 395, 0.030, 0.20, 2.3, t);
  makeOsc(ctx, dest, 'sine', 196, 0.025, 0.20, 2.2, t + 0.2);
  break;
}
```

**Sound design — sustained-voice:**
- **G4 (392 Hz) + detuned partner at 395 Hz** — the
  3 Hz difference produces audible BEATING that
  mimics the natural pitch-wobble of a human voice.
  Same trick as 106 nightmare's 64/68 Hz dissonance,
  but much quieter and slower.
- **Low G3 drone (196 Hz)** at +0.2s — adds pastoral
  warmth. Octave-below root. Fades in after the
  voice starts.
- **Slow attack (0.15-0.20s)** per oscillator — voice
  fades IN rather than announces. Cows don't notice
  a spoken "hello"; they notice the sung one
  because it arrives gradually.
- **Decay 2.2-2.5s** — longer than most cues; a
  shepherd's song isn't staccato.
- **Gain 0.045** — just under namesake (0.05);
  voice-level, not music-level.
- **Pure sines** — no triangle buzz; voice emulation
  depends on clean tones for the detune-beat to read
  as "singing."

## why this shape

- **First voice-emulation cue in the catalog.** Prior
  cues were bells, chimes, chords, noise bursts,
  spoken-name, resolution chords. Nothing
  impersonated a sustained human voice. Detune-beat
  is the low-cost trick that works.
- **Matches 138's prose.** "${founder2} is heard
  singing at the cowpen" — audio should read as a
  sung note, not a bell. Detune-beat reads as
  sustained voice when quiet.
- **Pastoral warmth from the G3 drone.** A pure G4
  alone would sound synthetic. Adding an octave
  below gives the sound BODY — the kind of resonance
  barn wood and cattle add to a real shepherd's
  voice.
- **Gain 0.045 < 0.05 (namesake) < 0.06 (founders).**
  Each composer cue has landed quieter than the
  previous. The "miss-able" invariant is getting
  more strictly honored as the catalog grows.
- **Pattern 2 (onFire).** 138 is in NARRATIVE_BEATS.
  Reuses the refactor from 123. No new schema.
