# 003 — the-photographer

**Challenge:** the-photographer — accepted: hunt for a cover-art
frame across time-of-day + season. Document what you had to do to
get it and what's ugly on the way there.

## setup

Loaded `Continue` save (Year 1 Day 15 Autumn, 3 citizens, 1 farm).
Built one house (hotkey 1); lumber-mill click didn't land on a
forest tile so only 2 buildings total. Population auto-grew 3 → 7
during interaction (housing works — silent answer to iter 002's
"how does pop grow?").

Used `G.dayPhase = N` to jump across the day (0–3600 cycle).
Tried `G.season='spring'` to compare foliage — **the override
doesn't stick**: HUD still reads Winter, snowflakes keep falling.
So "season" is authoritative somewhere upstream of the `G.season`
key; live flipping is not a valid photo-mode path.

## frames captured

- **Frame A — Winter dusk** (`dayPhase=2700`): settlement
  barely visible. Heavy tint flattens every color to a murky blue-
  green. Citizens read as 8px dots. Water holds its blue best of
  any element in frame. Falling snowflakes are the only lively
  detail.
- **Frame B — Winter "midday"** (`dayPhase=1800`): essentially
  the same tonal value as dusk. The curve between 1800 and 2700
  should be the game's brightest and darkest moment of the day;
  here they're within ~10% luminance of each other. **This is
  the biggest graphic finding.**
- **Frame C — Spring(-attempted) midday**: grass did brighten,
  beach sand warmed to gold at the south water edge, but overall
  lighting remained cool-moody. Snowflakes persisting from
  winter confirmed the season override didn't propagate.

No frame reached "cover-art quality." The closest was the
**south-east water + sand edge** at any hour — small diamond
water tiles, clean beach border, mountain silhouettes behind.
That composition is a real asset the rest of the palette isn't
hitting.

## what I noticed (graphics)

**Tonal compression across day/season.** Winter midday and
winter dusk are indistinguishable at a glance. The day-phase
lighting curve appears heavily damped — possibly a side-effect
of the historical night-floor raise documented in
`archive/LOOP_STATE.md` ("Night raised to 0.55 floor, was 0.3,
too dark to play"). The fix may have lifted nights by clamping
the whole curve upward, flattening the peak too. Needs a look at
wherever day lighting is computed (`render.js`? `main.js`?).

**Winter is too cold.** Not "cold-themed" — *underexposed*.
Every tile reads muddy. Compare to classic winter art that keeps
highlights bright (sun on snow, warm lit windows). Realm winter
loses the whole warm side of the palette.

**Particles don't respect live season state.** Minor; normal play
doesn't flip seasons instantly. But if you tried to build a photo
mode, this would bite.

**Composition is actually strong.** Dense tile detail, readable
building silhouettes at mid-zoom, horizon layered with trees /
mountains / water. The bones are there. Lighting is the thing
holding it back from cover-art.

**UX during photography:**
- `PAUSED` label center-top is correct for gameplay but blocks
  the composition center when you want a screenshot. A photo
  mode that hides HUD + pause label would help. Also pairs with
  marketing needs.
- Mission panel + minimap + build bar eat ~30% of the frame at
  1568×768. A "hide UI" toggle (`H` by convention) would
  unblock composition.

finding on its own — flagging only so a future tick doesn't
treat it as missing.
