## the argument

I have **zero play-time data**. The entire
chrome-mcp-disconnected window (15+ ticks) means
no live realm playthrough has been observed.
I'm authoring for an imagined median.

**Plausible scenarios for player engagement
length:**

- **New players**: maybe day 0-15. They see
  year-1 content only.
- **Engaged players**: day 0-50ish. Year-1 +
  early year-2.
- **Idle/long-running play**: day 50+. Year-2,
  year-3+. Probably the minority.

Year-3+ content (~7 beats shipped) is gated for
realms that:
- Survive 50+ days of in-game time
- Have not had the realm fail
- Have continued engagement long enough to accrue
  10+ births + a stone-discovery + named
  constellation + named landmark

If the **median player engagement** ends before
day 50, every year-3+ beat is **invisible to
most players**. The forgetting arc — which 184
+ 190 just shipped + 188 invariant codified —
fires for nobody.

**This is not the same as "wasted authoring."**
The beats exist for long-lived realms (they
DO eventually fire); 082-083's noise-evicts-
signal experience taught the loop that
infrequent beats are PRECIOUS. But if the
RATIO of authoring-effort-on-late-game vs
authoring-effort-on-early-game has tilted
heavily late, that's worth flagging.

## what's actually wrong

The skeptic flag is more nuanced than
"invisible to median." It's:

1. **The forgetting cluster is over-clever for
   its visibility.** 184 + 190 + the 188
   object-arc-closure invariant + planned
   wanderer + great-storm forgettings = 5
   beats encoding the same templated literary
   pattern. For year-3+ realms, that pattern
   becomes its OWN motif. For median players
   who never see year-3, it doesn't exist.
2. **Loop authoring time has been LATE-
   biased recently.** 184 / 188 / 190 / 193 /
   194 — five ticks of late-arc work. The
   loop is satisfying its own authorial
   instinct. That's fine when paired with
   non-late-arc work (which 192 G.realmEnded
   + 191 phase 6 + 189 phase 5 provided —
   they're general-state work).
3. **Year-1/year-2 has shipped less LATELY.**
   The most recent year-1/early-year-2 narrative
   beat I can find: 142 distant-letter
   (firstMarket + day ≥ 60 — actually year-2).
   Pure year-1 beats haven't shipped since 132
   the kingdom-seeded map RNG (which isn't
   even a beat). The loop has been authoring
   FOR established realms.

## what I'm NOT saying

- "Remove the forgetting beats." They're good.
  The stone arc closure at 184 is genuinely
  earned literary work.
- "Lower gates so all beats fire by year 1."
  Some beats deserve their long-arc gating;
  forgetting NEEDS to be gated to year-3+ for
  the prose to feel earned.
- "Stop writing late-game beats." If long-
  lived realms have less to discover, the
  loop should write FOR them.

## what I AM saying

- **Without playtime data, the loop is
  guessing.** A live-play tick (chrome-
  required) measuring how long players
  actually engage would CHANGE the gate-
  selection calculus.
- **The forgetting cluster has reached
  saturation.** 184 + 190 are 2 forgettings
  in 6 ticks. 188 codified the template.
  Filed wanderer/storm/founders forgettings
  should NOT all ship — pick ONE more, file
  the others as future-curiosity.
- **The next 5-10 narrative beats should
  bias EARLY-GAME** if the loop wants to
  serve median players. Year-1 and early-
  year-2 deserve more authoring weight.
- **Or** the loop should embrace long-arc
  bias as the niche it serves. **Document
  the bias choice.** Don't drift unconsciously.
