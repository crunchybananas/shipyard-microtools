# 002 — the-30-seconds (baseline)

**Challenge:** the-30-seconds — accepted: establish a baseline of
what realm feels like in its current state before any new loop
work touches it.

## what

Served `docs/realm/` on :8889, loaded in chrome-mcp (tab 1347743638),
played for ~30 seconds starting from the first frame after
clicking New Game (Easy, Peaceful, default kingdom name).

The 30s arc:

- **T+0** → Landing page: title "REALM", subtitle "A Settlement
  Builder", difficulty tabs, 5 scenarios, "New Game" / "Continue
  Year 1 Day 7 · 3" (save detected), 3 stat cards. Clean. Easy to
  find the primary action.
- **T+3** → Tutorial tip appears: "Your settlers need food! Build
  a Farm first. Click Farm below (or press 2)." The Farm button
  glows in the build bar. The "Welcome to Realm" banner
  overlaps my 3 starting citizens center-map.
- **T+8** → Clicked Farm → hotkey badge flashed → click on a
  grass tile placed a farm. Farm sprite appeared immediately.
- **T+12** → Resources updated: wood 100→116 (+16, ▲4/day),
  food 107→174 (+67, ▲40/day). "Build a farm" side goal
  strikes through with a checkmark animation.
- **T+14** → Tutorial advances: "Now build a Lumber Mill on a
  forest tile for wood. Click Lumber Mill (or press 3)."
- **T+30** → Stopped. Still had full control. A citizen was
  visibly walking to the farm. `Saved` indicator flashed
  top-left (autosave).

## what I noticed

**Strongest moment**: the select → place → resource-jump →
strike-through → next-step signal loop that fires in the first
~15 seconds. That's the kind of first-action-has-consequences beat
most city-builders don't land. Credit where due.

**Onboarding gaps visible in 30s**:

1. **Population growth is unexplained.** "Reach 10 population
   (3/10)" is the top mission and the most prominent number in
   the HUD. But the tutorial says nothing about *how* pop grows.
   A novice sees 3/10 and wonders: do I wait? do I build? do I
   recruit? (Actual mechanism is housing; never signposted.)
2. **No placement-preview before first click.** After selecting
   Farm, my next click placed it. In that ~1 second I had no
   ghost sprite, no cost diff, no "valid/invalid tile" shading. I
   intuited it worked; a true novice might click fearfully.
   *Maybe this IS working and the screenshot timing missed it —
   needs a second look.*
3. **Welcome banner overlaps starting citizens at T+0–3.** Minor
   visual clutter, but it covers the *one thing* a novice wants
   to see: their starting people. Banner could fade faster, or
   sit in a different zone.
4. **HUD symbol density is high.** Top-right has ~10 icon
   buttons (smiley, speaker, music, trophy, chart, beaker/flask,
   book, monitor, ...) with no labels visible. Their meaning
   probably reveals on hover — didn't test. Worth a
   tooltip-coverage audit in a later tick.
5. **"Skip tutorial" button reads defensive.** It's right there
   under tip 2/10. Good for experienced players. But its
   presence *on the first tip* might undersell the tutorial to
   someone who needs it.

**What was NOT a problem** (worth naming, so future ticks don't
re-fix it):

- Load time: game rendered < 1 second from New Game click.
- Readability: resources/HUD text all legible at 1512×807.
- Camera position: island was centered, no blank screen.
- Responsiveness: click → action feedback was instant.
