# 055 — constant-shift (dream cadence 14 → 10)

**Challenge:** `constant-shift` — accepted. First
use in 54 ticks. Non-fixer variety move; the-fixer
is at 41% share and four never-used `[code]`
challenges were available (constant-shift,
dead-code-detective, name-giver, refactor-only
re-run). Constant-shift offered a specific testable
thing to change.

053 flagged mid-game chronicle as thin: one beat
every 14 days. 054 expanded the dream pool (8→10
threads). 055's question: does the combined 054
pool let us shorten the cadence without repetition
regressing?

Target: `G.day % 14` → `G.day % 10` in
`checkDreamBeat` (story.js:411-412). One line of
meaningful shift.

## the fix

```js
// before
if (G.day < 14) return;
if (G.day % 14 !== 0) return;

// after (Loop 055)
if (G.day < 10) return;
if (G.day % 10 !== 0) return;
```

Plus a 6-line comment block naming the trade-off
for future ticks to reason about. No other changes.

## verification (chrome-mcp on :8891 via 049 SW)

Play-tested with the same 053/054 methodology:
8 buildings, pop=15, scan days 10→155.

| metric            | 053 (14d) | 054 (14d, +pool) | 055 (10d, +pool) |
| ----------------- | --------- | ---------------- | ---------------- |
| total dreams      | 11        | 11               | **15**           |
| image slots       | 33        | 33               | 45               |
| unique images     | ~17       | 20               | **26**           |
| repeated images   | ~11       | 7                | 12               |
| repeat ratio      | ~48%      | 35%              | 46%              |
| dreams day ≥ 100  | ~4        | ~4               | **6**            |
| first dream day   | 14        | 14               | **10**           |

### honest read

**Wins:**
- First dream arrives day 10 instead of 14. New
  realms feel the dream system 4 days sooner.
- 36% more dream fires per-realm-lifetime (15 vs 11).
- Late-game dream count up (6 vs 4 after day 100).
- Unique image count up 30% (26 vs 20).

**Losses:**
- Repeat ratio returned to 46%, close to the pre-054
  baseline of 48%. The pool-expansion gains were
  partly reabsorbed by faster cadence.
- Specifically: day 100 fires 3 repeated images
  back-to-back. Day 140 has "snow arriving on the
  wrong side" surfacing for a third time in the run.

### qualitative check

Sampled dreams across the run. The early dreams
(days 10, 20, 30) feel fresh — players new to the
system haven't seen any of the images before. The
*late* dreams (100+) repeat frequently; by that
point most players would be pattern-matching rather
than reading closely.

Cadence=10 trades early-game freshness for late-
game recycling. In a game with the 043 nightmare
landing around day 150 as the big late-game beat,
the mid-game dream redundancy is less costly than
it reads on paper — the nightmare will dominate
player attention by then anyway.

One real dream at day 10 for the TestRealm:

> At dawn the realm wakes to shared dreams: the
> well filled with stars instead of water; then a
> fog that knows every name and refuses to say
> them; then the first word each child in the
> realm will speak, all in unison.

Three images, three distinct threads (water,
weather, children — weather and children both new
from 054). This is the kind of dream 054's pool
expansion was made for.

## trade-offs the tick did NOT resolve

- **Thread exhaustion at late-game.** Fixing fully
  would need pool size = dream fires per realm ×
  3 slots / repeat tolerance. For a 155-day run
  at cadence=10 that's 45 image slots and a
  tolerance of maybe 20% repetition, suggesting
  a pool of ~37 images. 054's 10 threads × ~4.5
  images = ~45 images total, so there's room —
  but `requires()` gates narrow surfaced pool.
  A future `the-archivist` or `the-profiler` tick
  could measure thread-surfacing frequency and
  expand specifically where pool is thinnest.
- **Cadence vs special moment.** A dream at every
  10 days makes dreams less rare. 053's reflection
  "the nightmare at day 150 is the best narrative
  moment in the run" partly depends on dreams
  being notable. 055 hasn't made dreams cheap, but
  it's moved a needle.
- **Didn't try 12.** A compromise cadence of 12
  would split the difference. But constant-shift
  asks for ONE shift, play-tested. 12 is filed
  as a follow-up IF 10 proves too frequent in
  feedback.

## what I did NOT do

- **Revert to 14 on finding the repeat increase.**
  The challenge is to *document consequences*, not
  to revert at the first unwelcome result. If the
  loop or user decides 46% repeat is too high, the
  revert is a single-line follow-up tick.
- **Touch anything else in story.js.** Tempting to
  add a 3rd thread to compensate, or adjust
  thread-surfacing. Stayed tight to "change one
  constant, measure, report."
- **Touch 044's time-aware lens.** Seasonal boost
  still applies unchanged. Special-date intros
  still apply.
