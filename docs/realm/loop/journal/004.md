## what I noticed

**1. The curve is season-invariant.** All four seasons produce
byte-identical rows. Seasonal look is entirely driven by
`SEASONS[...].tileShift` (per-channel RGB offset applied to tiles),
not by a global luminance multiplier. My iter-003 claim that
"winter underexposed" was a lighting-curve issue was wrong — it's
the blue tint (`winter.tileShift = [-10, -5, +15]`) dominating.

**2. The plateau is rectangular and enormous.** From t=0.167 to
t=0.583, the game is at exactly 1.0 for 10 simulated hours — 42%
of every day is *flat* max brightness. There's no peak/fall-off;
no golden hour built into the curve; no sun-angle sweetness.

**3. Night is nearly flat too.** Last 6 hours (t=0.75 → 1.0)
drops from effLum 0.813 to 0.759 — a 6.6% change that takes a
quarter of the day. You don't feel night *progressing*.

**4. The 003 "midday ≈ dusk" claim is partially rehabilitated.**
effLum at t=0.5 = 1.0 vs t=0.75 = 0.813 — a 19% drop that
*should* be perceivable. So either (a) my 003 screenshots
captured a moment before `G.dayPhase` settled, or (b) the blue
multiply tint is so dominant that the 19% luminance drop gets
perceptually swallowed. Both are plausible — good for a future
`the-pixel-critic` or `the-color-eye` to disambiguate.

**5. The night floor is 0.55.** `archive/LOOP_STATE.md` noted an
old fix: "Night raised to 0.55 floor (was 0.3, too dark to play)."
That's still the live value. The fix wasn't surgical — it raised
the *floor* but didn't re-shape the rest of the curve. Plausibly
why the game feels "always daytime" or "always lit."

## what the data does NOT say

- Whether the plateau *should* be narrower (gameplay might prefer
  long midday; this is a design decision, not a bug).
- Whether the 0.55 floor is correct (could be right for this game
  and wrong for a photo-mode screenshot; context-dependent).
- What the tint overlay looks like in practice — `effLum` is a
  rough proxy, not a measured pixel sample. A more rigorous
  next-tick could snapshot the canvas and compute actual pixel
  luminance.

## ideas generated → ideas.md

- Golden-hour peaks: replace the flat 1.0 plateau with a
  symmetric peak around t=0.4
- Seasonal sun-angle: winter peaks at ~0.9, summer at ~1.05
- Canvas pixel-luminance sampler (more rigorous than `effLum`)
- Configurable night floor (expose `G.debug.nightFloor` knob)
- Night-progression curve: let night dim further past 0.76 then
  recover, so the back half of a day *feels* like one
