## the change

`docs/realm/js/trade.js:executeTrade` — 2 lines added:

```js
const merchantMult = G.namedCharacters?.merchant ? 1.05 : 1;
const received = Math.round(amount * p.rate * merchantMult);
```

Named merchant (from 034's `ensureMerchant`, fires on first
market) multiplies trade return by 1.05. Applies to ALL 4
trade partners uniformly (albion/orisk/thalos/meridian).
+5% per trade.

## why this shape (vs 101's +10%)

- **Smaller bonus than teacher's +10%.** Trades are a
  higher-volume interaction than research; a long-running
  realm can execute dozens of trades per year. Cumulative
  +5% compounds to meaningful gold over time without being
  dominant. Teacher's +10% on a ~10-tech research tree is
  smaller-total-impact; +5% per trade across 30+ trades
  ~= similar cumulative.
- **Multiplicative vs additive.** Trade has one
  multiplicand (rate) rather than 101's additive
  school+teacher stack. Multiplicative on the result is
  the natural shape for trade economy.
- **Math.round, same pattern as original.** Small trades
  may round to the same integer (see orisk case below).
  Acceptable — mechanic shines on larger trades.

## verification (chrome-mcp on :8891 via 049 SW)

6-case matrix (merchant present vs absent, across 3 trade
partners):

```
Partner   Trade             Rate    Base      +Merchant   Delta
Albion    20 wood→gold      2       40        42          +2 (+5%)
Orisk     20 food→iron      0.3     6         6           0 (rounded)
Thalos    20 stone→gold     1.5     30        32          +2 (~6.7%)
```

- **Albion/Thalos**: clear +5% in integer output (40→42,
  30→32)
- **Orisk**: rate 0.3 × 20 = 6; × 1.05 = 6.3 → rounds to 6.
  Small trades can round out the bonus; larger orisk trades
  (say 40 food) would show +1.

All correct math. `node --check js/trade.js` → OK.
