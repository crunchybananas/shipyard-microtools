## why this shape

- **Closes 101 filed (100 ticks).** The single
  most-aged named-character mechanic. 101's
  filed text: "bard → +5 happiness baseline
  when named. ~3 lines. [economy.js or happiness
  tick]." Shipped exactly per filing.
- **Additive, not multiplicative** — break from
  the 101/102/105+153 multiplicative pattern.
  Bards raise spirits; they don't *amplify*
  buildings or systems. The flat baseline
  expresses character: "the realm feels better
  with a bard around" — independent of how many
  taverns or churches it has. Establishes
  **additive baseline pattern** as a sibling
  to the multiplicative `*Mult` boilerplate.
- **`50 + bardBonus`** rather than `50 +
  hBonus + bardBonus`. Placement matters:
  bard is part of the BASELINE not part of
  the building-derived bonus. Reading the
  formula left-to-right you see 50 (base
  spirit) → +bard (raised spirit) → +
  buildings (institutional spirit) → +offset
  (research/seasonal) → -overpop (stress).
- **Clamped invisibly at extremes.** With the
  min-10/max-100 clamp, bardBonus only matters
  in the open range. Saturated-happy realms
  (≥95) won't see the +5; collapsing realms
  (≤10) won't either. The mid-range (10, 95)
  is where the bonus carries weight — exactly
  where pacing decisions live.
- **No chronicle beat.** Per 101 invariant:
  034's character-intro already announces the
  bard arrival; mechanics ship silently
  thereafter. Matches teacher/merchant/smith
  conventions.
- **5 of 6 named-cast now mechanic.** Mayor
  (civic-unlock, larger scope) + rival (raid-
  difficulty +10%, adversarial framing) remain
  filed. The mechanic-cast is functionally
  complete for cooperative characters; only
  the structural-unlock (mayor) and adversarial
  (rival) frames need separate treatment.
