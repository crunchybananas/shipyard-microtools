# 007 — the-novice

**Challenge:** the-novice — accepted: pretend I've never played a
city-builder. 90 seconds. What's confusing? Where do I stall?
Which tooltip saves me? Dual-purpose this tick: verify 006's
welcome-toast fix in the wild.

## setup

Hard-refreshed `http://localhost:8889/`, clicked New Game with
defaults (Easy, Peaceful). Pure UI interaction only — no
`window.G` console access, no DOM poking. Chrome-mcp extension
dropped connection at ~T+70s, ending the session one step short
of placing the lumber mill. Enough data captured.

## the 90-second arc (as novice)

**T+0 landing** — Clean. Title + 1-sentence pitch + difficulty/
scenario picker + "New Game" primary. I click New Game.

**T+2 in game** — Map appears fast. Three citizens visible
center-map. **No welcome modal covering the citizens.** 006's
fix has landed in the wild. First-impression frame is clean.
(The toast may have appeared top-center in the first 2s and
already faded — didn't catch it in a screenshot, but the fix
achieved its stated goal: the map center is never obscured by
a decorative notification.)

**T+5 tutorial registers** — Bottom-left tip: "Your settlers
need food! Build a Farm first. Click Farm below (or press 2)."
The Farm button in the build bar glows. This is strong
direction. Press 2.

**T+7 placement-preview grid appears** — Translucent grid
overlays eligible tiles. I *notice* it this time. (Iter-002 and
iter-003 missed it due to screenshot timing; iter-006 confirmed
it exists. Now I can report that as a novice it's **subtle** —
the grid is low-contrast against the bright grass, and I only
really see it because I'm looking for it.)

**T+12 place farm** — Click grass tile → farm appears → farm
sprite animates in → "Build a farm" goal strikes through with
checkmark. *Satisfying.* Named character hover shows on a
citizen ("Ada Pike — Looking for work"). **This is a flavor
beat I hadn't noticed before; citizens have names.**

**T+20 tutorial advances** — "Now build a Lumber Mill on a
forest tile. Click Lumber Mill (or press 3)."

**T+25 mistake: I click the map before pressing 3.** The
placement-preview stayed active from the previous Farm
selection, so my click placed a *second farm* instead of doing
nothing or warning me. The tutorial said "Click Lumber Mill
or press 3" but didn't block the map from accepting the
previously-selected Farm placement. **This is a real novice
trap.** Wood rate flips to ▼18/day — I'm now losing wood
because of a farm I didn't mean to place.

**T+40 recover** — Press 3, click a clearer grass area (meant
forest but overshot). Screenshot at T+65 shows two farms and
a lumber-mill gesture. Extension drops at T+70.

## confusions / friction (as novice)

1. **Scenario descriptions missing on landing.** What does
   "Peaceful" vs "Military" vs "Merchant" mean? No hover, no
   caption. I guessed based on the emoji. A one-line each would
   save the guess.
2. **`2/10` under the tutorial tip is ambiguous.** Is it "2 of
   10 tutorial steps" or "2/10 resources"? I inferred steps but
   a tooltip label would remove the doubt.
3. **Placement selection persists across tutorial steps.** The
   tutorial telling me to "build lumber mill" doesn't cancel
   the previous "Farm" selection. A novice who doesn't read the
   tip carefully will accidentally place another of the
   previous building. This caused my mistake at T+25.
4. **"Reach 10 population (3/10)" unexplained.** Already filed
   in 002, still true. Building farms didn't move the number.
   I'd want a tiny hint: "(build housing to grow)".
5. **Small dot markers on tiles have no tooltips.** Red/blue/
   pink dots scattered across the map. Resources? Forage? I
   don't know. I hovered — nothing. Not tested at length, but
   a novice wants to know "what are these pretty dots."
6. **Hot air balloon is ambient decoration, reads clickable.**
   Balloon drifts across the map center. Gorgeous. But as a
   novice I want to click it — is it a quest? a resource? (It's
   purely decorative per LOOPS.md.) An ambient object that
   LOOKS interactive creates a dead click and a "did I miss
   something" worry.

## tooltips that saved me

- The **tutorial bottom-left** is the single most-useful UI
  element in the first minute. Without it I'd have flailed.
  It's well-placed (doesn't block the map), readable, and
  action-oriented.
- The **goal strikethrough** at T+12 was a satisfying signal
  that I'd done the right thing. Equivalent to the "cha-ching"
  of a well-designed mobile game.
- **"Ada Pike — Looking for work"** is a flavor moment that
  made me smile and want to keep playing. Naming is cheap and
  disproportionately powerful.

## 006 fix — in-the-wild verdict

The welcome toast did not obstruct the map at any point in this
90-second session. 002's original complaint is resolved in
practice. **No regression observed on the other in-session
toasts** I'd expect — tutorial tips, the "Farm built!" ticker,
the goal strikethrough all render in their own zones. I did
*not* trigger a danger toast (which would be the toast type
that most "needs" center-attention), so the regression question
there is still open — will need to be caught by a `the-gamer`
or `the-pessimist` tick.

## ideas generated → ideas.md

- Scenario descriptions on landing (one line each, revealing
  on hover or always-visible below the button group).
- Change `2/10` in the tutorial tip to `Step 2 of 10` or
  equivalent labeled progress.
- Cancel building-selection on tutorial-step advance, OR: flash
  the new tutorial target in the build bar to draw the eye
  away from the old selection.
- Resource-dot tooltips: hover any tile-decoration → "🍓
  Berry bush: forageable by citizens."
- Disambiguate decorative ambient objects (balloon, birds,
  etc.) — either make them subtly interactive (click for
  flavor line) or add a cursor-change hint that they're NOT.

## what I noticed (graphics)

The **placement-preview grid is low-contrast.** It renders as
a pale translucent green over already-green grass tiles.
Against darker terrain (forest / mountain) it's visible;
against grass it nearly disappears. A novice might not notice
it at all — I missed it in 002 and 003. A higher-contrast
edge (brighter green outline, or a subtle pulse) would make
the system more discoverable.
