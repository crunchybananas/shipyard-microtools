## why Lenwood

Probed candidate kingdom names via
their kHash distribution across the 6
spec'd variant types:

```
candidate     | distribution | idx-0% | distinct
Lenwood       | 3,2,2,3,1,3  | 0%     | 3 palettes (1/2/3)
Verdant       | 1,2,2,1,3,1  | 0%     | 3 palettes
Salt          | 3,2,2,3,3,3  | 0%     | 2 palettes
... (others all 17-50% idx-0)
```

Lenwood: 0% idx-0 hits + 3 distinct
palettes spanning idx 1, 2, and 3.
It's the kname that BEST balances
the cohort:

Pre-293 idx distribution (4 knames ×
6 sprites = 24 samples):
- idx 0: 7/24 = 29% (over-represented)
- idx 1: 4/24 = 17% (under-represented)
- idx 2: 6/24 = 25%
- idx 3: 7/24 = 29%

Post-293 (5 knames × 6 sprites = 30
samples):
- idx 0: 7/30 = 23% (close to 25%
  expected)
- idx 1: 5/30 = 17%
- idx 2: 8/30 = 27%
- idx 3: 10/30 = 33%

idx 1 still under-represented but
overall closer to uniform. Most
importantly, no SINGLE kname now
hits 50% idx-0 (Norrith's pre-293
issue).
