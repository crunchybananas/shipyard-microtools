## what blacksmith.svg expresses that canvas struggles with

- **Hammer mid-strike on anvil.** Canvas has
  the hammer as a separate prop against the
  wall; SVG places ANOTHER hammer ON the
  anvil reading as "active forging happens
  here." Two hammers = two slots: working +
  resting.
- **Iron tongs on wall.** Canvas blacksmith
  has hammer + anvil but no tongs. Tongs are
  the second-most-iconic blacksmith tool;
  SVG adds them hanging from a wall hook.
- **Ash pile with ember dots.** Canvas
  forge window glows but the ground beneath
  is plain. SVG adds an ash ellipse with
  bright ember dots — makes the forge feel
  WORKED-IN.
- **Sparks rising above forge.** Canvas
  forge is contained within the window;
  SVG adds 3 small bright dots rising out
  through where the chimney draws — implies
  airborne sparks during a strike.
- **4 progressive smoke wisps.** Canvas
  blacksmith has no chimney smoke. SVG
  ships dark coal-smoke (vs house's
  lighter wood smoke) rising with
  size-progression to imply velocity +
  cooling/spreading.
- **Stone-block pattern via `<pattern>`.**
  Canvas does horizontal mortar lines via
  for-loop; SVG declares once + applies to
  walls AND chimney for visual cohesion.
