## method

For each of 20 buildings × 4 daylight levels
(dawn=0.4, midday=1.0, dusk=0.5, night=0.25):

1. Call `G.debug.renderBuildingIsolated(type,
   { daylight: dl })`
2. Quantize the 100×140 canvas to 3-bit per-channel
   buckets
3. Drop the grass-green background bucket
   (`#40a040` → `2_5_2` quantized)
4. Record top-3 non-background color buckets

80 canvases total. The full table is noisy but
yields clear cross-phase patterns.

## the HIGH finding

**`renderBuildingIsolated` does NOT apply the
012/017 night-tint multiply overlay.**

Proof: castle palette at midday and night:

```
castle midday:  #808060:373 | #606040:311 | #606060:299
castle night:   #60a040:524 | #a0a080:439 | #a08060:395
```

Both are earth-tone-dominant with minor count
variance. Compare to what the main render produces
at night: 012's indigo/amber night-tint multiply
would shift the whole palette toward indigo. My
sampled "night" canvas shows no such shift.

Why? The night-tint overlay lives at `render.js:
2515-2549` inside the top-level `render()`
function, NOT inside `drawBuilding`. It's a
whole-canvas multiply applied AFTER all sprites
are drawn. The isolated-building helper
(052) calls `drawBuilding` directly, which bypasses
the tint.

This means **054/055's color-eye-at-buildings
audits through the helper will never catch
tint-related issues**. The helper shows buildings
in perpetual midday regardless of the `daylight`
parameter's numeric value.

What DOES change with `daylight` in the helper:
- **Shadow length** (render.js:2827: `sunAngle =
  Math.abs(daylight - 0.7) * 3`) — shadows grow
  ~3× longer at dawn/dusk, ~2× at deep night
- **Sprite globalAlpha** at daylight-dependent
  fade (for partial buildings)
- Nothing else

Farm confirms this perfectly:

```
farm dawn:    #202000:440 | #402000:113 | #604000:104
farm midday:  #202000:440 | #402000:113 | #604000:104
farm dusk:    #202000:440 | #402000:113 | #604000:104
farm night:   #202000:440 | #402000:113 | #604000:104
```

**Identical across all 4 daylights.** Farm has no
shadow (it's a ground rhombus), so the one
daylight-dependent property doesn't move. Every
count is bit-for-bit the same.

### fix shape

Two options for a future tick:

1. **Apply night tint inside the helper**: after
   `drawBuilding`, replicate the 012/017 multiply
   block from render.js:2515-2549. +20 lines in
   renderBuildingIsolated. Pro: helper becomes
   complete for tint audits. Con: duplicates the
   tint math.
2. **Extract the tint into an exportable
   function** that both render() and
   renderBuildingIsolated call. Clean refactor,
   ~15 lines. Pro: single source of truth. Con:
   larger scope.

Filed both. Option 2 is better; pairs with
062's NARRATIVE_BEATS consolidation energy.

## the MEDIUM finding

**Tower and Barracks have very close midday
palettes** (confirms 046 source-read):

```
barracks midday:  #408040:485 | #608080:176 | #404060:139
tower midday:     #408040:581 | #606080:217 | #606060:210
```

Both dominated by same grass shadow (`#408040`),
both have mid-grey secondaries (`#606080` vs
`#608080`). The 046 silhouette-test found these
two share the "wide stone block" silhouette
family — this confirms it's a palette family
issue too. At distance, a barracks and a tower
next to each other would be visually
indistinguishable without the tower's crenellated
peak poking above.

Not urgent (crenellations ARE distinct), but
filed.

## additional observations

- **Schools always have warm plaster** (`#c0a060`,
  `#c0a080`) prominently. 015's schoolhouse voice
  carries through the sprite consistently.
- **Well's palette is almost all background
  and sky-bleed**: `#408040 | #60a040 | #c0c0a0`
  — the tiny well silhouette doesn't dominate its
  100×140 canvas. Note: well is one of the
  shortest sprites (peakY=77 in 052's profile).
  The helper may need a tighter bounding-box for
  low-profile buildings.
- **Farm is effectively a background tile**:
  its top colors (`#202000, #402000, #604000`)
  are all dark earth — the scarecrow (a 1-pixel
  thin stick at y-14) is lost in the palette
  count. For silhouette audits the farm is a
  near-zero signal building.
