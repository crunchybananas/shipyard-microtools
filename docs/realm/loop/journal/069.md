## first finding — chrome-mcp throttles hidden tabs severely

MANIFEST documents this: "MCP tabs are always
'hidden' — Chrome throttles rAF. The game batches
60 ticks per call to compensate; use G.speed=4
and wait if you need time to pass."

In practice:

```
T0 (start):       day 1,  gameTick 0
T1 (30s real):    day 12, gameTick 10140
T2 (90s real):    day 14, gameTick 12540  (+2 days in 60s)
```

Extrapolating: ~60s of real-time = ~2 game-days at
speed=4. A full season cycle (30 days) would need
15 minutes of real observation. A 200-day realm
would need ~100 minutes.

**This means the programmatic `G.day = N` approach
isn't laziness — it's the only workable method in
chrome-mcp.** 069 is the first time the loop has
DIRECTLY experienced this throttling, and it
clarifies why 053/057/068 went programmatic.

Filed as noted; not a bug, but a real constraint
that 020's letter didn't anticipate.

## second finding — TRIPLE chronicle fire at season change

This is the real HIGH finding of 069, and it's
something NO programmatic audit could catch because
it requires the main.js season-advance path to run.

Day 8 (summer begins), the chronicle shows THREE
back-to-back entries for one conceptual event:

```
day 8 | event     | "☀️ Summer begins! (Season 2)"
day 8 | season    | "Long days and warm winds. The fields grow golden."
day 8 | misc      | "Children play at the well. 'Summer is for growing,' says the schoolteacher."
```

Tracing the code:

- `event` fire is from `notify()` in main.js:302 at
  season transition, which routes through
  `notifications.js:68` → `chronicle(text,
  tagMap[type] || 'misc')` with type='event'.
- `season` fire is main.js:310 direct
  `chronicle(seasonTexts[G.season], 'season')`.
- `misc` fire — haven't traced yet, but from the
  "schoolteacher" voice + seasonal tie, it's
  likely from `advisor.js` or a similar tip-system
  consulted at season boundaries.

Three chronicle rows for one moment is noise. The
`event`-tagged one is literally the UI toast ALSO
written to chronicle, which is redundant with the
`season`-tagged entry that has better prose.

060's archivist audit didn't catch this because its
programmatic run never drove real season
transitions (updateSeason needs main.js loop).
069's live play surfaced it in under 90 seconds.

### fix shape

Either:
1. Don't chronicle the notify-toast for season
   events (cleanest — the toast is ephemeral UX,
   the `season` beat IS the chronicle memory).
   1-line edit: add `season` to a skip-list in
   notifications.js:68.
2. Make the notify→chronicle path tag-aware so
   season notifications don't duplicate the
   direct `chronicle('season')` call.
3. Drop the direct main.js:310 chronicle and only
   keep the notify-route.

Option 1 is simplest and most conservative — keeps
both code paths, just prevents the notification
router from writing to chronicle when the direct
code already will.

## fourth finding — out-of-season dream imagery

Day 10 dream (summer): `"At dawn the realm wakes
to shared dreams: snow arriving on the wrong side
of the year, unmelted by morning..."`

The **snow** image is from 054's `weather` thread.
We're in summer. 044's seasonal boost for summer is
water+market. So the thread's OWN images aren't
season-filtered; only boosts are. A summer realm
can dream of snow.

This is a design question: is "a summer dream of
winter" thematically wrong (breaks immersion) or
right (dreams are supposed to be strange)? 044's
special intros and thread boosts both work
correctly. The image is merely inside the weather
thread's normal pool.

I LEAN "right" — "snow arriving on the wrong
side of the year, unmelted by morning" is
explicitly a summer-context dream image (it says
the wrong side of the year). The dream system is
doing what 039 intended: images that feel wrong,
in a good way.

Leaving as-is. Noted for any future review.

## sixth observation — the pacing feels tolerable

At speed=4 with chrome-mcp throttling, days pass
at roughly 1 per 15 real seconds. In a real browser
(not MCP tab), that would be much faster. But
even throttled, the idle-player experience in
60-90 seconds hit:

- 3 milestone beats (founding + house + farm)
- 1 dream
- 1 triple-season-change cluster
- 2 misc elder-sayings

That's density. The 055 cadence shift (10-day
dreams) + 054 pool + 057 beats means an idle
player has something to read every minute or two
of real time. The loop's narrative work is
pacing-visible.
