## silent stretches

16 stretches of 8-9 consecutive silent days in the
200-day run. Breakdown:

```
Day ranges with gaps: 3-9, 11-19, 21-29, 31-39,
41-49, 51-59, 61-69 (etc.) — exactly the 10-day
dream cadence from 055.

Silent-stretch median length: 9 days
```

055's cadence shift (14→10) reduced these from ~13
to ~9. With 10-day dream cadence + rare lifetime
beats, the narrative surface has a steady-state
"one beat every 10 days" heartbeat, punctuated by
occasional echoes and the 2 once-per-realm beats.

**Is this right?** Arguable. The realm has no
narrative activity between scheduled dream fires
unless events (combat/stranger/plague) trigger.
A pure-peaceful realm on a 10-day heartbeat is
quiet by design, but the player has no signal the
chronicle is alive during the quiet stretches.

## coherence findings

### GOOD — the layers compose

The 200-day sample shows all non-story-driven
surfaces (dreams/nightmare/stone/echoes) firing
cleanly alongside the milestone-driven ones. No
collisions. 059's echo correctly references only
sources ≥14 days old, which preserves the
"memory" framing.

044's time-aware lens is quietly working:
ArchivistRealm was run on 2026-04-21 (spring), and
the dream sample shows harvest+water images
dominating early dreams ("nets of starlight",
"the well filled with stars") — seasonal boost
is visible.

### GOOD — tag governance

Of the 13 tags, no two systems fight for one tag:

- `character` is shared by story.js ensures +
  events.js bard-arrives, but both are valid
  character-introduction beats. Semantic overlap
  is intended.
- `raid` is shared by story.js first-raid +
  events.js tribute-war. Both are raid-related.
  OK.
- `event` is events.js-only. OK.
- `milestone` is the broadest tag; probably should
  be. No issue.

### MEDIUM — echo source pool misses 2 tags

`_ECHO_SOURCE_TAGS` at story.js:648 includes:

```
milestone, victory, character, birth, dream,
nightmare, stone, raid
```

But NOT:
- `event` — the cloaked-stranger / plague / bard
  beats never get echoed
- `season` — season transitions never get echoed

Adding them is trivial (set push). Filed. The
`event` beats in particular have memorable content
(the stranger that "vanished by nightfall" is the
kind of thing a bard would sing of).

### MEDIUM — day-2 cluster

Programmatic test placed everything on day 2; real
play spreads this out. But even in real play, a
player who power-builds can hit 10-15 milestone
beats in a single game day. The chronicle panel's
UX at day 2 is a wall of "first X / first Y / first
Z." Not a bug, but worth noting: if the user ever
complains of "chronicle spam at early game," that's
why.

### MEDIUM — narrative-dead silent stretches

9-day stretches of zero chronicle activity between
dream fires. For a peaceful realm with no combat
or events, the chronicle feels static for ~90% of
game-days. 059's echo system adds ~3-6 echoes per
200 days, which helps, but they're also dawn-gated
so they cluster at dream-adjacent days.

Fill options (filed as ideas):
- Random mid-day "elder saying" beats (would live
  between dream fires)
- Weather-triggered beats ("first snow" / "summer
  storm")
- Production-milestone beats ("the realm's
  thousandth loaf of bread")

### LOW — 053 mis-identified a "missing" idea

053's filed "season-transition beats" idea
(`[story.js], ~25-30 lines`) is NOT actually
missing. main.js:304-310 already defines:

```js
spring: 'The snows melt. Green shoots push through
        the thawing earth.',
summer: 'Long days and warm winds. The fields grow
        golden.',
autumn: 'Leaves turn amber and crimson. The harvest
        is upon us.',
winter: 'Frost grips the land. Fires burn low in
        every hearth.',
```

fired via `chronicle(text, 'season')` on season
change. 053's programmatic play didn't trigger
season advance (updateSeason needs main.js loop,
not checkStoryBeats alone), so 053 saw zero season
tags and thought the idea was open.

Implication: **a programmatic audit can miss
surfaces that only main.js drives.** Future ticks
surveying "what chronicle writes exist" should
grep, not rely on what fires in a scripted test.
This audit grepped, caught it.

### LOW — chronicle cap vs long-session realms

Chronicle caps at 300 entries (story.js:26). At
current density (~56 entries / 200 days +
low-rate events), a realm that runs 1000 days
would still be well under. But if a future
narrative layer boosts fire rates (e.g., character-
aware echoes, weather beats), the cap becomes
relevant. Not acting on it now; noted.

### LOW — echo hasn't hit event beats

Side-finding from the tag-distribution analysis:
in an events-dry realm, the echo sources are
narrow (milestone/dream/character dominate). If
events.js fired during a realm's life, those beats
are eligible for echo only AFTER the 059 fix to
add `event` to `_ECHO_SOURCE_TAGS`. Small
correctness win from a 1-line change.

## gaps that SHOULD be filled

The narrative surface covers: birth/first-build/
growth/named-characters/dreams/nightmare/stone/
echo/season/combat-death/events. It does NOT
cover:

1. **Research completion** — "The realm has
   mastered Masonry" etc. A whole progression
   axis is silent.
2. **Happiness rises/falls** — no beat when
   happiness peaks or crashes. A real
   emotional-state signal missing.
3. **Long-sustained peace** — if no raid for 50
   days, no "the realm has known peace for…"
   beat.
4. **Long-sustained famine/crisis** — similar,
   opposite.
5. **Production records** — "first harvest yields
   100 food" kind of beats.

All filed below as new ideas — none had been
previously.
