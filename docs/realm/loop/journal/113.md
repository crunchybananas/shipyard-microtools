## the build

### 1. `docs/realm/js/audio.js` — new `stone` case

Added after `requiem`:

```js
case 'stone': {
  makeOsc(ctx, dest, 'sine', 659, 0.07,  0.01, 1.3, t);
  makeOsc(ctx, dest, 'sine', 988, 0.055, 0.01, 1.5, t + 0.08);
  // High shimmer overtone
  makeOsc(ctx, dest, 'sine', 1976, 0.015, 0.01, 0.9, t + 0.12);
  break;
}
```

**Sound design — discovery-coded:**
- **659 Hz** (E5) fundamental — brighter register than
  106 (C2) or 111 (G3). "Up" in the vocal range.
- **988 Hz** (B5, perfect fifth above E5) at +80ms —
  ascending pair, consonant. "A thing is shown."
- **1976 Hz** (B6 shimmer) at +120ms, gain 0.015 —
  faint sparkle, reads as "something glints."
- **Pure sines.** No triangle/square buzz. Clean.
- **Short attack 0.01s** (chime struck), **mid decay
  1.3s** (fades cleanly; not haunting like requiem).
- **Gain 0.07** — slightly quieter than nightmare/
  requiem; a CHIME, not a proclamation.

### 2. `docs/realm/js/story.js:checkStoneBeat` — fire the cue

## why this shape

- **Pattern 1 (dedicated function).** Stone is a
  check-function (`checkStoneBeat`), not a
  NARRATIVE_BEATS entry. Pattern 1 fits naturally; 112's
  spec specifies this.
- **Higher register than prior cues** — contrast table
  now spans C2 → G3 → E5 (4 octaves). Future cues have
  clear space at mid-range.
- **Shimmer overtone is novel** — 106 had a "ghost
  bell" at 384 Hz but it was a slow fade-in. 113's
  shimmer is a sparkle at +120ms (quick-in, quick-out).
  "Glint on stone" is the imagery.
- **Gain 0.07 < 0.10 invariant ceiling.** Well within
  112's "miss-able" rule.
- **Consonant fifth** — thematically opposite 106's
  minor-second dissonance. The realm's three rare cues
  now cover the emotional triangle: tension
  (nightmare), finality (requiem), discovery (stone).
- **3rd cue satisfies 112's "3 is a pattern" rule for
  Pattern 1** (nightmare, stone). Pattern 2 still at 1
  (requiem). No `onFire` callback refactor pressure.
