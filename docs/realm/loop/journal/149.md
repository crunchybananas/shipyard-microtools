## the build

### 1. `docs/realm/js/audio.js` — new `great-storm` case

```js
case 'great-storm': {
  makeNoiseBurst(ctx, dest, 0.03, 2.0, t, 400);
  makeOsc(ctx, dest, 'sine', 72, 0.05, 0.4, 2.5, t);
  makeOsc(ctx, dest, 'sine', 144, 0.025, 0.5, 2.2, t + 0.3);
  break;
}
```

**Sound design — memory of a storm, not the storm:**
- **Low-band noise burst (400 Hz cut, 2.0s, 0.03
  gain)** — muffled wind. The 400 Hz lowpass is
  aggressive: no high-frequency crispness at all.
  Sound travelling through time loses highs first
  (physics + memory metaphor). This reads as
  "distant" even without spatial reverb.
- **D2 sine rumble (72 Hz, 0.4s attack, 2.5s decay,
  0.05 gain)** — the storm's body. Slow attack is
  the key choice: the rumble RISES rather than
  announces, like a memory forming while someone
  speaks it. No other cue attacks this slowly
  (shepherds was 0.15-0.20s, nightmare 0.5s but
  fast-onset at low gain).
- **D3 overtone (144 Hz, 0.5s attack, 2.2s decay,
  0.025 gain) at +0.3s** — adds depth. Octave above
  root keeps the harmony coherent without
  brightness.
- **Gain peak 0.05** — equals offering (125); in the
  quieter half of the catalog. A storm REMEMBERED
  is not a storm HAPPENING.
- **No noise in the mid/high register** — this is
  the first cue to deliberately exclude content
  above ~500 Hz. The absence is the signal.

## why this shape

- **First audio cue for an event-tag beat.** Prior
  8 cues covered milestone/character/requiem/stone.
  147's beat is tag: `event` (fits the echo-system).
  This tests whether the onFire dispatch is truly
  tag-agnostic — it is. No dispatch changes needed.
- **First cue to occupy the sub-bass register.**
  Catalog had C2 (nightmare 64 Hz triangle) but
  nothing at pure-sine sub-bass. D2 72 Hz sine fills
  that slot; storms LIVE in sub-bass in real life
  (thunder rumble, wind through large objects).
- **Matches 147's prose.** "Some of the realm
  remember a great storm: ${storm}. The children
  born after do not believe it." — a cue needed to
  signal MEMORY, not PRESENCE. Muffled = memory.
- **Distinct from 106 nightmare.** Both are low-
  register, but nightmare is dissonant triangle with
  4 Hz beating (anxious, unstable); great-storm is
  harmonic sine octave (distant, stable). Same
  frequency neighborhood, opposite affect.
- **Pattern 2 (onFire).** 147 is in NARRATIVE_BEATS.
  One-token addition; reuses 123's refactor.

## what I did NOT do

- **Thunder crack** — a distinct lightning-strike
  transient could add drama, but the beat is about
  *memory* of a storm, not *presence*. Thunder would
  read as "now," undermining the text's "children
  born after do not believe it."
- **Per-variant variation** — 147 has 5 storm
  variants (wind / rain / east-storm / bronze sky /
  lightning-struck-nothing). Audio is the same for
  all. Per-variant cues would require a way to pass
  the variant index from story.js to audio.js; the
  onFire schema is string-form only. Filed as
  future-onFire-payload work if it gains a 2nd
  caller.
- **Live-audition.** Extension disconnected.
- **Founder content.** Moratorium still running
  (tick 3 of 10). 149 is structural (audio cue for
  existing beat), not a new founder surface.
