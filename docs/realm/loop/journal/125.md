### 1. `docs/realm/js/audio.js` — new `offering` case

```js
case 'offering': {
  // D4 fundamental held throughout
  makeOsc(ctx, dest, 'sine', 293, 0.05, 0.03, 1.8, t);
  // F4 (minor 3rd) — short, decays as F#4 arrives
  makeOsc(ctx, dest, 'sine', 349, 0.04, 0.03, 0.35, t);
  // F#4 (major 3rd) — arrives at +0.3s as resolution
  makeOsc(ctx, dest, 'sine', 370, 0.045, 0.05, 1.5, t + 0.30);
  // A4 (perfect 5th) — at +0.3s, completes major triad
  makeOsc(ctx, dest, 'sine', 440, 0.035, 0.05, 1.5, t + 0.30);
  break;
}
```

**Sound design — lifting-coded:**
- **D4 fundamental** (293 Hz) held throughout. Matches
  neither 111's G3 nor 113's E5; occupies a middle-
  register slot absent from the catalog.
- **F4 minor-3rd at t** (349 Hz, short 0.35s decay)
  — the beat OPENS minor. Sibling echo of 106
  nightmare's dissonance.
- **F#4 major-3rd at t+0.3s** (370 Hz, 1.5s decay) —
  arrives as the F4 decays. The ear perceives the
  pitch lifting from F4 to F#4. ~0.3s feels like "a
  held breath before release."
- **A4 perfect-5th at t+0.3s** (440 Hz, 1.5s decay) —
  completes the D-major triad. The full chord sustains
  together for ~1.5s before fading.
- **Pure sines** — consistent with 111/113/115.
  Contrasts 124's noise-based first-snow.
- **Gain 0.05 peak** — between stone's 0.07 and
  first-snow's 0.04. Fits the "miss-able" ceiling.

## why this shape

- **Sibling to 106 nightmare** — same minor-interval
  opening, but lifts where nightmare stays
  dissonant. The two beats' roots (C2 and D4) are
  4 octaves + one major-2nd apart; they occupy
  different registers but share a minor-interval
  gesture. If a player heard both in sequence, the
  offering would feel like a reply.
- **D major** specifically — the brightest of the
  keys-relative-to-root. 111 was G-minor rooted; 113
  was E-major implied; 115 was C-minor. 125 lands in
  D major. Each cue has claimed its own key center.
- **Short duration (1.8s)** — offerings are quiet
  moments. Not ceremonial like 115's 3-note founders;
  not final like 111's 5s requiem bell. Quickest cue
  by decay.
- **Pattern 1 (dedicated function)** — fires from
  `checkOfferingBeat`. Not NARRATIVE_BEATS-dispatched
  because 079 designed offering with cross-system
  gates (stone_found AND happyPeakActive AND
  probabilistic 15% fire) that don't fit the table's
  simple trigger predicate. Pattern 1 is the right
  shape.
