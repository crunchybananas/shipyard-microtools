## why these 8

Each one was chosen because the loop's actual behavior
has outgrown the original pool's coverage:

- **the-composer** — audio is now a first-class axis
  (audio-surfaces.md exists; 3 cues shipped). The pool
  had no audio challenge. `the-composer` gives the next
  audio tick a deliberate slot instead of always being
  `the-fixer closes filed cue`.

- **the-balancer** — 3 character-mechanics shipped; a
  play tick measuring cumulative impact was already
  filed (101). Promoting it to a challenge signals that
  cumulative-balance checks should happen periodically
  as more mechanics land.

- **the-cartographer** — every graphics tick since 003
  has been about BUILDINGS. The map (tiles, biomes,
  decorations, water) has been implicit in every
  screenshot but never a deliberate audit target. An
  underused axis deserves a challenge slot.

- **the-migrant** — `the-novice` is "first time
  playing." `the-migrant` is "played once, came back
  after a break." Returning-player stall-points are
  genuinely different from new-player stall-points.
  Post-MVP game, this matters.

- **the-typographer** — HUD overflow (041), tag-filter
  UI (078), chronicle filter chips (078) have all been
  typography-touching but piecemeal. A dedicated
  typography audit would catch issues the building-
  focused ticks keep skipping.

- **the-interrupter** — `the-idle-player` plays at
  speed×4 and checks back. `the-interrupter` plays,
  tabs away, comes back. The FIRST 5 seconds of
  re-engagement are what matters (did notifications
  pile up? did something change I need to catch up
  on?). Different experience.

- **the-critic** — 25+ chronicle systems, ~90+ prose
  beats shipped. The voice has evolved. No tick has
  ever read all of it side-by-side for consistency.
  Would catch tone drift 060/073/075/080/084/091/104
  can't see (those were taxonomic, not prose-quality).

- **the-re-shipper** — explicit counterweight to
  `the-fixer` monoculture concerns. Rather than "close
  the oldest," `the-re-shipper` must pick an idea open
  20+ ticks and either ship OR drop it. Forces
  quality-over-quantity on backlog health.

## what this is NOT

- **Not a challenge deletion.** The original 34 stay.
  Some (`the-30-seconds`, `the-art-director`) have been
  picked only once in 100+ ticks but that's fine —
  occasional picks are the point.
- **Not a MANIFEST update.** This tick touches
  `prompts/challenges.md` only. The MANIFEST's cadence
  bands, mix targets, Evolution mandate all still hold.
- **Not a renaming.** The 8 new names follow the
  `the-<persona>` pattern of the originals; no style
  break.
- **Not a reorganization.** Original `### personas`,
  `### code`, `### graphics`, `### review`, `###
  surprise` sections untouched. New expansion in its
  own labeled section at the end so readers can see
  where the original set ends.

## what I did NOT do

- **Re-classify the expansion by tag.** Could have
  split the 8 across personas/code/review sections
  inline. Kept together in a labeled expansion section
  so future reviewers can see what was pool-original
  vs. added-later.
- **Promote any ideas.md entry to a challenge.** None
  of the 8 are directly copied from ideas.md; they're
  all abstracted from observed patterns.
- **Add a challenge that references the REQUIEM tag or
  Lira arc specifically.** Too narrative-specific; the
  challenge pool should stay general-purpose.
- **Add an `anti-pattern` challenge.** "Do something
  deliberately bad" is fun but not productive in a
  loop that wants to ship.
- **Document the tick-114 expansion in the MANIFEST
  Meta section.** Minor; challenges.md self-documents
  via its "added tick 114" subhead.
