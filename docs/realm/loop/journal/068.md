## method

Same 20-building × 4-phase matrix as 066:

```
midday: daylight=1.0, dayPhase=1800  (no tint)
dawn:   daylight=0.4, dayPhase=792   (t=0.22, rose)
dusk:   daylight=0.5, dayPhase=2808  (t=0.78, amber)
night:  daylight=0.25, dayPhase=180  (t=0.05, indigo)
```

80 canvases sampled via `G.debug.renderBuildingIsolated`
with explicit `dayPhase` now that 067's param
exists. Top-3 buckets per canvas, 3-bit channel
quantization, **INCLUDING the background** this
time so we can see the grass tint shift.

## the core shift

**The whole canvas tints, not just the building.**

Midday background color (grass): `#40a040`, ~12,500
pixels out of ~14,000 (89%).
Dawn: grass becomes `#406020` — count drops slightly
to ~12,100 because shadows lengthen.
Dusk: grass becomes `#208020` — darker green-amber.
Night: grass becomes `#206020` — darkest.

The multiply tint darkens everything proportionally,
which IS what 012/017 intended. The consequence is
that at night, **grass takes up more visual
dominance than it did at midday** because it's now
all one dark-green hue, while the building's native
palette shrinks into small minority counts.

## finding — THE RESIDENTIAL CLUSTER BLURS HARDER AT NIGHT

046 / 066 flagged the house≈tavern silhouette blur
at midday. 068 finds this gets WORSE at night.

Peaked-roof + chimney residential cluster, TOP-2
non-bg colors at night:

```
house:    #406020:632 | #204020:391
tavern:   #406020:652 | #204020:398
lumber:   #406020:672 | #204020:358
bakery:   #406020:780 | #204020:397
market:   #406020:628 | #204020:387
windmill: #406020:714 | #204020:403
granary:  #406020:719 | #204020:378
```

**Seven buildings. Same two colors. Same rough
counts (±~40%).** Under night tint, house / tavern
/ lumber / bakery / market / windmill / granary all
produce nearly-identical pixel signatures. The
per-building detail that distinguished them at
midday gets compressed into the tint's narrow color
band.

051's tavern-flag silhouette fix still helps at
DAWN/MIDDAY/DUSK (shape differentiates). At night,
shape is what you read silhouettes by — but colors
are what the pixel-sampler sees. A pixel audit
picks house=tavern=bakery at night. A human player
would still distinguish them by shape against the
shared dark palette.

**Verdict:** not a rendering bug, but a REAL
observation about audit methodology — night-tint
palette audits should measure against SILHOUETTE
shape, not just color histograms. 046's source-read
or 052's peakY-profile data remains more useful at
night than color-eye.

## finding — CASTLE AND CHURCH RETAIN IDENTITY

Buildings with strong native distinguishing colors
survive the tint better:

```
castle night: #406020:1458 | #806040:1031 | #406040:608
church night: #406020:1762 | #606020:299  | #806040:232
bakery night: #406020:780  | #606020:258  | #806040:256
schools night:#406020:?    | #a06040:375  | #a06060:...
```

Castle's `#806040` (warm tan) shows a HIGH count
(1031) even under tint — it's a color far enough
from grass that the multiply preserves discriminable
signal. Church similarly retains some warm tones.
Bakery and school both surface `#806040` or `#a06040`
in their top-3.

Cover-art rule emerging: **buildings whose native
colors are PERCEPTUALLY FAR from grass-green
(warm oranges, reds, yellows, beiges) are
night-legible. Buildings whose native colors are
close to grass-green (grey stone, green-tinted
wood) blur.**

## finding — FARM IS ALMOST TINT-INVARIANT

```
farm midday: #40a040:12581 | #202000:439 | #402000:120
farm dawn:   #406020:12603 | #200000:579 | #402000:164
farm dusk:   #208020:12563 | #200000:556 | #402000:150
farm night:  #206020:12573 | #200000:626 | #402000:228
```

Farm's earth-tones (`#202000`, `#402000`) are
already VERY dark at midday. The tint can't darken
them much further (multiply's floor is `darkness
* 0.7` capped). So farm's actual sprite pixels are
nearly identical across phases — the grass
background is what shifts, not the farm itself.

Practical meaning: **farm is a building you
always see, regardless of lighting**. At midnight
or noon, your farms look the same. No other
building has this property.

## finding — THE HELPER NOW MATCHES LIVE GAME TINT

I forced castle through the helper at each phase
and compared the top colors to what I'd expect
from the 012/017 hue-lerp math:

```
dawn   (t=0.22):  kR=80,  kG=140, kB=160 → amber-ish
dusk   (t=0.78):  kR~147, kG~124, kB~132 → blend amber→indigo
night  (t=0.05):  kR=80,  kG=140, kB=160 → same as dawn (pre-dawn arc)
```

Castle dawn shows `#406020` (warm leaning) top,
dusk shows `#208020` (cooler green), night shows
`#206020` (deepest). The dawn-rose ≠ night-indigo
distinction that 012 introduced is now visible in
isolated renders — which it wasn't in 066.

067 verified. Re-opens color-eye as a usable tool
for post-tint audits.

## what 066 got wrong / right

**Right:** flagged the helper coverage gap (the
HIGH). Without 067, all tint audits would be
blind.

**Wrong:** 066 claimed "tower ≈ barracks palette
overlap at midday" was a real blur. At midday their
palettes differ slightly (barracks has `#608080`
mid-grey, tower has `#606080` cooler grey). The
signal is modest but present. Where the TRUE blur
lives is at NIGHT, when 7 residential buildings
all compress to `#406020 | #204020`.

Reclassifying: the tower vs barracks blur is
LOW-MEDIUM (shape differs by crenellations). The
residential night-blur is MEDIUM — but only a
concern if audits matter at night, and at night
players navigate by window-glow, not silhouette.
