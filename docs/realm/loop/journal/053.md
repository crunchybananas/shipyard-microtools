## method

chrome-mcp on :8891 (049's SW). Built a programmatic
playthrough since chrome-mcp can't realistically
click through a full session:

1. Seeded a realm with all 14 researchable techs
   unlocked + 999 of every resource
2. Placed ONE of every building (23 types) on
   grass tiles spiraling out from the center
3. Fast-forwarded the game 155 days by iterating
   `G.day` through dawn + midday phases and
   calling `story.checkStoryBeats()` + `enh.enhUpdateAll()`
   per day
4. Audited chronicle + story flags + named
   characters at the end

Not a *real* play in the sense of rendering every
frame, but close to one: all the narrative side-
effects fired in their correct order, the nightmare
seeded day was hit, dream thread pool was exercised,
and every first-build path executed once.

Between day 2 and day 155, the realm sees **12
scheduled narrative beats total** — one every 14
days plus the nightmare. No character development
after day 2. No mid-game events. No seasonal
chronicle beats. Just dreams at the dawn cadence
and the one nightmare.

This is fine EXCEPT the dreams start to repeat.

the realm feels stuck in a particular mood.

The 039 dream system was calibrated for
*occasional* encounter. At 14-day cadence, a 155-
day run hits 11 times. The 8 threads × 3 images
average starts recycling quickly once you see
more than 4 dreams.

### LOW — Default kingdom name is hardcoded

Checked: new-game initializer leaves `G.kingdomName`
as `"Realm"`. My override `G.kingdomName ||=
'CompletionistRealm'` did nothing because "Realm"
is already truthy. Consequence: **every default
game has the same nightmare day (150) with the
same named-character images**. First-time players
who pick no custom name see the identical
nightmare prose, playthrough after playthrough.

Could be a bug (nightmare feels less special if
it's the same every time), could be a feature
(players who discover this pattern get a "wait,
same three words" moment). Filed as noticed, not
flagged as fix. Worth a surprise-tick design
conversation.

### LOW — Season never advanced via `G.day` ticks alone

After 155 "days" of iteration, G.season was still
`spring`. `updateSeason()` wasn't being called
from my loop — the main.js game loop drives it,
not `checkStoryBeats`. So all 40 chronicle entries
got `season: "spring"` stamped on them even though
they span multiple seasons by day count (spring
→ summer → autumn → winter should cross ~day
30/60/90 in a standard scenario).

This is a programmatic-playthrough limitation, not
a game bug. Real play advances season via the tick
cycle. But it's a reminder that the-completionist-
via-script misses the season-change cascade
(window palette swap, the _t048 respawn, tileShift
reapply). Filed as a verification-helper follow-up.

### LOW — _t048 probably never ran in this playthrough

048's walker triggers on season-change. Since
season never advanced from "spring", the `_t048_
season !== G.season` condition stayed satisfied
on the first tick and then never re-fired. I
can't inspect `_t048` directly (module-local) so
can't confirm, but the *most likely* state is
either "never spawned" or "spawned once at start
of spring and walked off the edge by day 155."

The player-facing effect is fine either way —
attentive players see it or don't. The verification
gap is what matters: there's no console tool to
*check* whether 048's element is currently on the
map.

Follow-up: `G.debug.t048()` getter that returns
the internal state (or null). Would let future
verification ticks confirm behavior.

## audit: narrative surface health at tick 053

The realm now has FIVE procedural / semi-procedural
narrative layers:

1. **First-build beats** (022/026) — data-driven,
   one-shot per building. Missing: tower, well,
   chickencoop, cowpen.
2. **Named characters** (034) — fire on specific
   building construction. Mayor on house, smith on
   blacksmith, bard on tavern, merchant on market,
   teacher on school, rival on tower. All 6 fired
   cleanly.
3. **Collective dreams** (039) — every 14 days at
   dawn. 8 threads, per-slot hashes. Pool
   repetition becomes audible after ~5 fires.
4. **Time-aware dream lens** (044) — seasonal
   boost + special-date intros. Seasonal boost
   unverifiable in this playthrough (no season
   change). Special-date intro (today is 2026-04-
   21 = spring) should have biased toward harvest/
   water — and indeed 9 of 11 dreams had harvest
   or water images as the first or second slot.
   Works.
5. **Once-per-realm nightmare** (043) — fires on
   seeded day. Composed cleanly with named-
   character pool (6 chars) and was the most
   narratively distinct beat in the 155-day span.

What's NOT layered in:
- No reaction to game state beyond buildings/
  named-chars. Happiness doesn't affect dream
  tone. A realm under raid-stress dreams the same
  peaceful dreams as a flourishing one.
- No chronicle tag for `season` transitions. The
  game has seasons (via updateSeason) and the
  first-build beats carry the season in metadata,
  but no dedicated "spring arrives," "autumn
  harvest" beat ever writes.
- Nothing fires at the END of things — no "last
  house falls" for a realm dying, no "castle
  crumbles" for defeat. The chronicle is
  origin-heavy and death-silent.
