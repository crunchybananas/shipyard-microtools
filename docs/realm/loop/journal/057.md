## why these texts

- **tower** — chose "eyes reach the horizon" to
  match `watchtower`'s lookout role. The existing
  barracks beat is "Recruits drill at dawn";
  tower complements as the defensive sibling.
- **well** — "cold sweet water meets the light"
  references the dug-well image. Matches the
  "element-revealed-to-light" voice used in lumber
  ("hums to life") and granary ("sealed against
  weather").
- **chickencoop** — "new kind of dawn chorus"
  references the rooster-at-dawn trope without
  saying "rooster." Keeps the voice spare and
  slightly magical.
- **cowpen** — "milk and hide from patient beasts"
  parallels "grain will become flour" (windmill) —
  function stated, tone soft.

No `after` hooks (034's named-character trigger).
Could be added later if these buildings should
introduce new characters (e.g. a shepherd for
cowpen), but none of the 6 existing named-character
slots naturally maps.

## what I did NOT do

- **Add `after` triggers** for chickencoop / cowpen
  to introduce a shepherd/herder character. Would
  require expanding the 6-slot `G.namedCharacters`
  system. Out of scope for a fix tick; filed.
- **Add road/wall beats.** Per 053's reasoning:
  these get placed many times, a single `firstRoad`
  beat would fire once then stop making sense as
  the road network grows. A different mechanic
  (e.g. "first road connects two buildings" or
  "500 tiles walled") would be more appropriate.
- **Touch BUILDING_FIRST_BEATS's ordering.** The
  4 new entries sit at the end so the table reads
  chronologically (022's 5 at the top, 034's 3
  with after-triggers, 057's 4 at the bottom).
- **Replace the inline `firstTavern` /
  `firstBarracks` / `firstChurch` conditionals
  further down** (story.js:~197-213). They have
  `ensure*` calls that the table pattern doesn't
  yet support via the simple `after` field — ok
  for now. A future refactor-only tick could
  unify.
