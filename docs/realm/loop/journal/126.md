Simulated scenario: **user switches tabs at day 20

## timeline of loss (2-min absence)

### seconds 0-5 after blur

**Toast** at `notifications.js:49-51` fades after **2.8
seconds**. Any toast that fired during the absence is
gone.

### seconds 7-9.2 after each feed item

**Activity feed** at `notifications.js:97-99`:
- Fade at 7s
- Exit animation at 8.5s
- DOM removal at 9.2s

Activity feed keeps **max 5 items** (line 94). If more
than 5 events fire during absence, oldest drop silently.

### seconds 5+

**Log badge** at `notifications.js:108` hides after 5
seconds. The user can't tell from the badge-state alone
that anything new happened.

### 2 minutes of realtime at G.speed=1

Approximately **2 game-days** pass (1 day ≈ 60s at
speed 1). At G.speed=4 that's ~8 game-days.

## what PERSISTS

- **Notification log** (`notificationLog` array in G,
  capped at 50 entries) — accessible via Activity Log
  (`L` key or the button). Shows the last 50 events
  with timestamp.
- **Chronicle** (capped at 300, eviction-immune for
  `nightmare/stone/victory/requiem`) — accessible via
  the chronicle panel. Records story beats.
- **Missions panel** — top-right, shows current
  objectives. Persistent UI.
- **Minimap** — shows current realm state always.
- **Resource counters** — HUD updates in real-time
  (but the player only sees the CURRENT values, not
  the DELTA since last view).
- **All persistent save state** — realm didn't pause.
  Citizens grew older, raids may have come and gone,
  buildings may have burned, population may have
  changed.

## what's LOST (the re-engagement gap)

1. **All toasts that fired during absence** — ~3s
   fade means anything mid-absence is gone.
2. **Activity feed items older than ~10s** — nothing
   remains that's specifically marked "just
   happened."
3. **Resource deltas** — the player sees "Wood: 45"
   but has no way to tell it was "Wood: 20" two
   minutes ago. No "+25 wood" or "−10 food" on
   return.
4. **Life-critical events** — citizens could have
   died; raid could have happened and been resolved;
   building could have burned. If the EVENTS fired
   toasts that aged out, the player has to CHECK the
   chronicle/log to reconstruct what happened.
5. **No "welcome back" summary.** Zero DOM handlers
   for `visibilitychange`, `blur`/`focus`, or
   similar (grep confirms: `Found 0 files` in js/
   for `visibilitychange|document\.hidden`). No
   pause-on-blur. No re-engagement UI.

## what the game DOES provide for catch-up

- Press `L` → Activity Log panel → scroll through
  last 50 entries. WORKS but requires active
  catch-up effort.
- Press `C` → Chronicle → see the narrative beats.
  Historical, but beats filter out UI noise.
- Read the HUD → see current state. No deltas.

This is an **accept-the-loss** design: the game
doesn't TELL you what you missed; it just resumes.
The player must CHOOSE to catch up.

## three concrete findings

### HIGH — no "welcome back" summary

For a 2-minute absence at speed 4×, ~8 game-days
passed. Events that fired: probably 5-15 notifications
(raids, births, food warnings, research completions,
etc.). The player returns to current state with NO
signal about what changed.

**Fix shape (~20 lines):** handle `visibilitychange`
event. On blur, record `G.blurredAtDay`, `G.blurredAtGold`
etc. On focus, compute deltas and render a
"Welcome back — 2 minutes (day 23-25): -12 food, +2
citizens, 1 raid" summary toast.

030 filed a "welcome back aggregated summary" idea.
It's still open. 126 confirms it.

### MEDIUM — activity feed doesn't distinguish "new since return"

If a user tabs back in, they see 0-5 feed items but
can't tell which are "old, from before I left" vs
"new, happened during my absence."

**Fix shape (~5 lines):** when visibilitychange fires
to visible, mark the current latest feed item; new
items get an "unread" class with subtle highlight.

### LOW — no pause-on-blur option

Some players prefer the game to pause when they tab
away. Currently no such option.

**Fix shape:** setting toggle in UI → on blur, if
setting enabled, `G.speed = 0`. On focus, restore
previous speed.

This is a player-preference axis, not a bug.

## observations about the DESIGN

**The accept-the-loss approach has merit:**
- Real-time city-builders are ABOUT time passing.
  Forcing the player to catch up fully would fight
  the genre's pacing.
- The game's state reflects "what IS now," not "what
  CHANGED." A city where a fire happened and got put
  out just looks normal.

**But the current shape drops too much information.**
The player has TOOLS to catch up (chronicle, log) but
no NUDGE. A returning player often doesn't know they
need to check.

030's filed idea is the right fix: a small, passive
toast on return. Doesn't interrupt, doesn't
auto-pause, doesn't change pacing — just says "here's
what changed while you were gone."
