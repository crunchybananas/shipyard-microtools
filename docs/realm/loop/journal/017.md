## the 013 diagnosis (for context)

The multiply-blend night overlay (`render.js:2515`) turns
OFF when `daylight >= 0.95`, which 004 showed is true for
42% of every day — the midday plateau. During that window,
the only seasonal chromatic input to tiles is
`SEASONS[...].tileShift`. Winter's `[-10, -5, +15]` made
midday look "uniformly blue-washed drained" per 013 Frame
5a, and, reversal of natural expectation, made winter-
midday darker-feeling than winter-dusk (where the amber
multiply at least opposed the cold tileShift).

## verification

Chrome-mcp, fresh New Game. Set `G.season='winter'`,
`G.dayPhase=1800` (midday), took a screenshot. Compared
mentally to 013 Frame 5a.

**Before (013 Frame 5a)**: "Muted blue-green grass, grey
stone, less-saturated water. Drained feel... uniformly
blue-washed flat-bright landscape."

**After (017)**: Grass reads *dormant cool* — still
distinct from summer's saturated green but no longer
drained. Water retains blue character. Sand at the beach
reads warm-tan. Trees and mountains keep their dark
silhouettes. **The scene still reads as winter** — cooler
palette, muted saturation — but not *tired*.

Also verified winter dusk (`dayPhase=2520`). The amber
multiply from 012 still lands warmly on the new cooler-
but-not-drained tiles: southeast grass reads warm-olive,
sand reads gold. Winter-midday vs winter-dusk remain
legibly distinct (013's acid test still passes post-tune).

Other seasons confirmed untouched via direct inspection:

```
spring: [0, 12, 0]   (unchanged)
summer: [8, 5, -5]   (unchanged)
autumn: [15, -5, -10] (unchanged)
winter: [-5, -3, 8]  (NEW)
```

No regression on non-winter states.

## what I noticed

1. **Chrome-mcp landing-page clicks drift on hard-reload**
   (third tick in a row this has happened — 016 saw it too).
   Worked around with `window.startNewGame()`. Not a realm
   bug; a testing-infra note for future ticks.
2. **The tune keeps winter *readable* as winter.** The
   temptation with a "muted → drained" finding is to
   over-correct and lose the seasonal cue entirely. Half-
   magnitude is enough of a preservation of the cool cast
   that the frame still says "winter" on first glance. A
   future `the-screenshot-critic` could re-check if the
   ratio feels right; the knob is a single array literal.
3. **Combined with 012, winter now has two chromatic
   voices**: cool-but-alive at midday (this tick), and
   warm-amber-over-cool at dusk (012). Summer-winter
   contrast is preserved; midday vs dusk contrast within
   winter is preserved. That's the structural shape the
   lighting story was trying to reach, landed in 6 ticks
   (003/004/005/011/012/013 → this one).
