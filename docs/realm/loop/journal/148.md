## why this shape

- **Acknowledgment WITHOUT naming.** 048 created the
  walker with the explicit "A later iteration can
  choose what this is — including NOT choosing."
  061 renamed the internal identifier but kept the
  player-facing anonymity. 148 adds a chronicle
  beat that EXPLICITLY declares "No one calls them
  anything." The not-naming becomes the beat's
  thematic content.
- **Tag: `misc` (reuse per 075).** `misc` is the
  existing tag for edge-of-voice observations
  (seasonal proverbs, elder sayings). Fits the
  wanderer's register.
- **No kingdom-hash variants.** Unlike prior
  surprises (5-variant, 20-variant pools), the
  wanderer beat is a SINGLE static string. Every
  realm's wanderer is the same wanderer — 048's
  design asserts this. Preserving that assertion
  means not varying.
- **Day ≥ 50 gate.** 048's wanderer spawns with 55%
  probability per non-winter season (3 seasons per
  year). Year 1 fire-expectation ≈ 1.65. By day 50
  (year 2, ~1.8 seasons past), expectation is ≥ 2.2
  — ~80%+ of realms have seen the wanderer at least
  once. Tick fires AS IF the player has seen it.
  Realms that haven't still get coherent lore.
- **Static string, no function.** text is a string
  not a (G)→string. Simplest possible entry.
- **Non-founder.** No `G.storyFlags.founder*`
  reference. Moratorium-compliant.

## what the wanderer surface adds

Before 148, the silent-walker existed ONLY as a
visual element — pixels moving across the map
seasonally. A player seeing it had no game-text
to anchor their observation. They saw a figure;
the game never acknowledged it.

After 148: the chronicle confirms the sighting
existed. The realm itself NOTICES. But —
critically — the realm still doesn't NAME. The
anonymity is thematic, not negligent. 048's
"unnamed-thing" challenge completes its cycle:
introduced (048) → renamed internal (061) →
acknowledged publicly without naming (148).

Three ticks across 100 ticks of realm life to
shape a single anonymous beat. Patient writing.
