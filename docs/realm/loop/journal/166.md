## why this shape

- **Different focal axis** from 148 (day figure
  crossing edge-to-edge) and 152 (night sight at
  the fields). 166 is **water-edge sound, paired
  with rain**. Sense modality: hearing, not sight.
  Time: evening, not full night/day. Location:
  water boundary, not interior fields or
  cross-realm path.
- **"Two voices" preserves ambiguity.** Reads as
  croaking + water lapping, OR as something
  stranger — the elder-silence response in line 2
  ("does not name the things speaking") matches
  148's "no one calls them anything" and 152's
  "no one asks them to describe it." Same family
  of restraint phrasing.
- **No "frog" word.** Honors the grammar
  invariant from 156 (template comment in
  story.js): the entity remains un-named in
  prose. Player can project frogs OR read it as
  a small mystery.
- **Static string, no variants.** 156 grammar
  rule honored: variants would imply plurality
  / collapse the singular ambiguity.
- **14-tick gap from 152.** 156 warned: "space
  them out — 3-4 in rapid succession would feel
  like queue-drain." 14 ticks is reasonable
  spacing; closer than 5 would be queue-drain.
- **Spring/summer + day ≥ 70 gate.** Frogs are
  vocal in warm seasons (per ecology / per
  enh.js:1822 frogs spawn dusk/night at sand-
  near-water tiles). Day 70 = mid-year-1, realm
  has settled. Most realms see this fire 1-2x
  in their lifetime if they live past spring of
  year 1.
- **Tag: misc.** 148/152 precedent. 075's reuse
  invariant honored.
- **From the 156 candidate list.** 156's filed
  candidates were owls/frogs/rams/trade-ships/
  hawks. Frogs picked because: water-edge focal
  (no other beat covers this); sound-based (no
  other beat is sound-focal); spring/summer
  gating (no other beat in this seasonal slot).
  Owls would have been redundant with 152's
  night-time. Frogs differentiates the grammar
  most cleanly.

  Considered: the prose says "after rain" so a
  weather-state gate could match. But that
  would require either tracking "recent rain"
  with a flag set at `rain.onEnd()` (events.js
  edit, scope creep) or a tight firing window
  during 2-day rain events. The static spring/
  summer + day gate captures the SEASONAL TIME
  when frog voices are heard; "after rain" in
  the prose is poetic-evocative, not literal-
  mechanic-bound. Same restraint as 148's
  "some seasons" (also poetic, not state-
  verified).
- **Audio cue.** 154 skeptic halt remains in
  effect. No `onFire: 'frog-voices'` even
