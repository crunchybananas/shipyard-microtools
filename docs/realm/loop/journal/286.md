### LOW — Avalon test-kingdom has degenerate variant distribution

Audit of variant kHash distribution
across the 6 spec'd sprite types and 4
canonical test realms (Avalon /
Norrith / Velar / Ashfall):

| kname | church | townhall | castle | granary | windmill | tower | idx-0 count |
|-------|--------|----------|--------|---------|----------|-------|-------------|
| Avalon | 0 | 3 | 1 | 0 | 1 | 1 | **2/6 (33%)** |
| Norrith | 3 | 0 | 0 | 3 | 0 | 2 | 3/6 (50%) |
| Velar | 3 | 2 | 2 | 3 | 3 | 0 | 1/6 (17%) |
| Ashfall | 2 | 3 | 3 | 2 | 2 | 0 | 1/6 (17%) |

**Norrith is the most degenerate**: 50%
of its sprites render in default
palette (no variant swap), so a player
in the Norrith realm sees 3-of-6
sprites looking exactly like a
hypothetical "no-variant" baseline.

Statistical expectation for 4 random
kHashes on 4-palette pools: 25% per
sprite at idx 0. Norrith hits 50% — 2x
expected. Possibly bad luck or a hash
clustering artifact at common kname
characters.

**Severity LOW** — variant-pool's
purpose is REALM-DIFFERENTIATION, not
INTRA-REALM diversity. Each kname
visually differs from siblings, which
is the actual goal. But the test
kingdoms aren't representative samples;
fixing this would harden visual A/B.

**Recommendation:** add a 5th canonical
test kingdom with better cross-sprite
distribution (or accept the existing
4 and document the LIMITATION at the
verify-variants header). Either is
fine; small-priority polish.
