## finding 1 — research panel: 5 of 13 techs clip off-screen

**Measured**: `.research-content` height 496px,
scrollHeight 879px. **383px of content below the
fold** at 1280×627 viewport.

**Visible on first open**: 9 of 13 cards
(Agriculture, Forestry, Masonry, Commerce in
FOUNDATIONS; Engineering, Metallurgy, Brewing, Baking
in ADVANCED; Animal Husbandry alone starts the next
section — and gets clipped mid-card).

**Below the fold** (requires scroll to see): Military,
Smithing in MASTERY, plus Architecture and Archery
in TIER 3.

**No visible scroll indicator.** The panel ends
where Animal Husbandry is half-cut-off; there's no
gradient, no "more below" cue, no visible scrollbar
on first look. A player who researches late-game
techs has to *know* to scroll. Silent hide.

**Secondary finding — empty columns in sparse tiers**:
MASTERY has only 2 techs (Military, Smithing) but
uses the same 4-column grid as FOUNDATIONS. Right
half of the row is empty. Same for TIER 3
(Architecture + Archery only). ~300px of horizontal
whitespace per sparse tier.

**Tertiary finding — label inconsistency**:
Section labels are **FOUNDATIONS / ADVANCED /
MASTERY / TIER 3**. First three are thematic; TIER
3 is a type label. One of these is not like the
others.

## finding 2 — HUD header: overflows at 1280 under stress

**At 1280 viewport**, default state fits (HUD
scrollWidth = 1280). But **under stress**
(long kingdom name + all counter-widgets non-zero
+ active research), scrollWidth rises to **1403** —
**123px overflow**.

The `#hud` at index.html:64 uses
`justify-content:center`. On overflow, content is
clipped *symmetrically* on both ends — the leftmost
and rightmost items disappear behind the viewport
edges silently.

**In the stress screenshot**:
- LEFT edge clips: 🪵 Wood (first), 🪨 Stone (second)
- RIGHT edge clips: 🛒 Trade, 📖 Chronicle, 🧊 3D
  toggle (last three `.hud-btn`s)

**User impact**: a mid-to-late-game player with a
normal-length kingdom name, iron mines, a barracks,
and a skirmish in progress cannot see their wood
count or access the trade / chronicle / 3D buttons
on a 13" MBA (1280 width).

**Items and their widths** (under stress):

| Element                          | Width |
| -------------------------------- | ----- |
| 🪵 Wood value                    | 46    |
| 🪨 Stone                         | 38    |
| 🍎 Food                          | 46    |

The two longest elements (kingdom 180px, date/season
195px) are expected to shrink is not graceful — at
1280, they hold their width and push other items
off-screen rather than truncating.

## what I did NOT do

- **Resize window** — `window.resizeTo` is blocked by
  browser security. Would need chrome-mcp's
  `resize_window` which I didn't use this tick (out
  of scope; measurements are width-deterministic).
- **Audit panels other than research** — Missions,
  Chronicle, Happiness, Stats, Trade, Achievements
  all have similar "panel overflows viewport"
  potential but weren't asked-about and 040 is
  user-scoped to research + HUD.
- **Fix anything** — the tick is an audit. Fixes are
  the next tick's job.

## concrete [code] follow-ups for next ticks

Filed in ideas.md:

1. **Research panel: add "more below" cue** — a soft
   gradient at the bottom edge of
   `.research-content` when scrollHeight >
   clientHeight. Even 3 pixels of darker gradient
   would signal scrollability.

2. **Research panel: collapse empty slots** — use
   `repeat(auto-fill, 140px)` or flex-wrap with
   flex-grow so sparse tiers don't leave 300px of
   whitespace. Or: condense MASTERY/TIER-3 to fit
   their cards left-aligned.

3. **Research panel: rename TIER 3** — pick a
   thematic name like "ROYAL" or "LEGENDARY" to
   match the FOUNDATIONS / ADVANCED / MASTERY
   vocabulary.

4. **HUD: prevent overflow clipping** — three
   complementary approaches, any one fixes it:
   (a) truncate the kingdom-display to max ~12
   chars with CSS `text-overflow:ellipsis` (a
   player can open a character/settings panel to
   see full name);
   (b) collapse the date-display at narrower widths
   ("Year 1 Day 1 🌱" instead of "Year 1, Day 1 · 🌱
   Spring · 🙂 50% 🟡");
   (c) move the right-side `.hud-btn` icons to a
   secondary row (or a "⋯ more" dropdown) below a
   breakpoint.

5. **HUD: overflow sentinel** — add a CSS rule that
   lights up an indicator when `scrollWidth >
   clientWidth`, so future ticks don't ship regressions
   silently. Could be a 1-line `outline: 2px solid
   red` via `@container` query.

6. **HUD: use `justify-content:space-between`** over
   `center` — or use flex `min-width:0` on individual
   items so kingdom/date shrink under pressure rather
   than holding their size and pushing neighbors off.
