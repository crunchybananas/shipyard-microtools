## why this shape

- **Different focal from 148.** The wanderer is a
  DAY figure crossing edge-to-edge (55% per non-
  winter season, moves in daylight). The 152
  surface is a NIGHT thing, seen by children, not
  confirmed by elders. Different time, different
  witness, different ambiguity texture.
- **No "ghost" word in prose.** 142 uses "winter-
  ghost" but only in news about a DISTANT realm
  ("their ridge"). Using it for our own realm
  would collapse the ambiguity the 031 entity
  deserves. "A bright shape in the fields" keeps
  interpretive space open (ghost / sprite /
  trick-of-moonlight — player projects).
- **Intergenerational frame without founder
  bookkeeping.** "The youngest in the realm" is
  a category, not a character. "No one asks them
  to describe it" echoes 148's "No one calls
  them anything" — same elder-silence pattern.
  Two misc-tag beats now share a voice: communal
  restraint toward ambient mystery.
- **Gated on `firstBirth`.** "The youngest" has
  to exist. `day ≥ 60` gives the realm time to
  settle; firstBirth confirms children are born
  (fires ≥ 1 citizen born from +1 birth event).
  Most realms hit both gates well into year 1.
- **Tag: misc.** 075 invariant — reuse tags.
  Already a misc precedent (148). `misc` tag
  fits elder-sayings register.
- **Static text, no variants.** 148's static-
  string restraint carried over. Variants would
  imply plurality; the ambient entity is singular
  by nature.

## the 148 / 152 pair

The two misc-tag acknowledgment beats are a family:
- **148** — the wanderer (day figure, travels). Gate:

elder-silence pattern. Together they form a **paired
