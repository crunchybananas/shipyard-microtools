**This is a lot of content for 60 real seconds of

elder/farmer flavor quotes, seasonal transitions, even
a fire disaster with recovery-feel copy. An idle
returning player has a chronicle worth reading.

### T+120s (second check-in)

```
Day 11 → Day 11 (!)
dayPhase 600 → 2520
Chronicle: 10 → 10 (no new entries)
Resources: food 589 → 608 (+19)
```

**Game effectively froze.** dayPhase advanced from 600
to 2520 (+1920) which is 0.53 of one game-day. At
speed 4, that's about 2 game-seconds per real-second
— many times slower than the first 60s interval.

Why the two intervals differed so dramatically:

```js
document.hidden: true
visibilityState: 'hidden'
```

Chrome's hidden-tab throttling. In the first 60-second
window I was running JS to set up the game; the tab
woke briefly each time. In the second 60-second window,
no JS was running — Chrome throttled `setTimeout` to
~1 fire/second even with the game's 60-tick-per-call
batching.

Per `archive/LOOP_STATE.md`: *"MCP tabs are always
'hidden' — Chrome throttles rAF and setTimeout. The
game loop batches 60 ticks per call to compensate but
may stall under heavy load."* That's the observed
behavior precisely.

## finding: this is an environment issue, not a bug

For a **real user** who sets speed=4 and tabs away:
- They come back expecting the game to have made
  progress.
- They don't usually tab back every 60s.
- A 5-minute tab-away returning to Day 25 (or ~5 game-
  days past where they left) is acceptable.

Chrome's hidden-tab throttling is system-wide — no
browser game overrides it. The "60-tick batching"
already in the game loop is the right mitigation; it
gets the game running at maybe 1/10 real speed in a
hidden tab, which is plenty for "I stepped away."

For **chrome-mcp testing** (this loop's environment),
the throttle makes idle-play findings unreliable. A
future the-idle-player tick in a non-MCP browser
(e.g. opened by the user directly) would produce
different evidence.

## what was fun

1. **The chronicle is genuinely alive during idle.**
   10 entries in the first 10 in-game days, including
   a disaster event (house fire) and a seasonal
   transition with flavor. An idle player coming back
   to read their chronicle would feel the realm has
   been *lived in*, not just paused.

2. **Births happen unbidden.** 4 of them in 10 days.
   Silent machine kept working.

3. **Disaster + flavor-copy pair works.** The fire
   event is a real game loss (house destroyed, build
   investment gone) AND comes with narrative framing
   ("Buckets are passed hand to hand"). The
   combination softens the loss into a story beat.

## what was missed

Things an idle return should surface but didn't:

1. **No visible "welcome back" summary.** If I'd
   really been away, I'd want to see "While you were
   gone: 4 births, 1 disaster, Summer began, +389
   food." The notifications log has the raw entries
   but no aggregated summary on return-to-tab.
   (Filing as idea.)

2. **No progress bar / eta toward next major event.**
   Next raid at Day 30, I'm at Day 11 → 19 in-game
   days away, but nothing visually tells me that.
   The `⚔ Nd` countdown I noted in 010 only fires at
   Nd ≤ 3. Could show at larger distances too.

3. **The house fire had no persistent marker.** A
   chronicle entry is good; but if an idle player
   scrolls past the toast, they might not see the
   impact on their map until they notice the missing
   house. A small "ruin" sprite where the burned
   house was would ground the fire in the world.

4. **The tutorial stays frozen.** Still "Step 2 of 10:
   Your settlers need food! Build a Farm first." —
   except I already built a farm. Checked
   `tutorialStep` — it's paused because the player
   didn't click-to-dismiss the "welcome" step 1 or
   the tutorial's `check()` function didn't fire for
   the right event. Known issue; 016 also flagged.
