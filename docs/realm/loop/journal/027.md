## the clicks

### click 1 — a citizen sprite (right-of-center)

Coord (1009, 358), visually on a citizen wearing a red
hat. Console probe after:

```
selectedCitizen: null
selectedBuilding: null
hoveredTile: {x:42, y:37}
```

**Dead click.** The click registered as a tile-level
interaction (hoveredTile changed) but neither the
citizen nor any building was selected.

Root cause (grep`ed after): `findCitizenAtClick` in
`input.js:58` uses a 15px world-space hit-radius around
each citizen. The rendered sprite visually occupies
more than 15px but the hit-box is tight. A kid's click
that *visually lands on* a citizen often misses the
narrow hit-box.

### click 2 — a mountain

Coord (1100, 160). Console probe: no state change
beyond hoveredTile update. Mountain tiles don't do
anything on click — no flash, no tooltip pop, no "you
clicked a mountain" hint. **Dead click with no feedback.**

Non-terrain interactions (forest trees, water, sand)
behave similarly per code inspection: the click lands
on a tile, hoveredTile updates, but if there's nothing
placeable or selectable there, silence.

### click 3 — HUD icon bar enumeration

I spent one kid-click trying to figure out what the
little icons top-right do. Did the grep on the DOM
instead (shortcut):

11 icon buttons, every single one has:
- An `onclick` handler (togglePopPanel, toggleResearch,
  toggleHappiness, toggleAmbientSound, toggleMusicBtn,
  toggleAchievements, toggleStats, toggleLog,
  toggleTrade, toggleChronicle, toggle3D)
- A descriptive `title` attribute (native browser
  tooltip on hover)

**This corrects 007 (the-novice)'s finding #4**, which
claimed "HUD has ~10 icons top-right with no labels
visible." Novice observed without hovering. Labels ARE
there — you just have to hover. That's not a tooltip
gap; that's the standard HTML `title` discovery model,
which works fine for mouse users but not touch users.

(A touch-device kid would indeed not find these labels.
That's the real version of the finding: the HUD
tooltip model doesn't serve touch.)

### click 4 — a flying bird (top of map)

Tried to click what looked like a bird/flock at top
edge. Chrome-mcp coordinate drift made this unreliable;
per code inspection, `G.birds`, `G.flocks`, `G.bats`,
etc. have no click handlers registered in `input.js`.
They're purely decorative.

**Intentional.** Ambient life is atmospheric, not
interactive. Kid still feels the lack — "the bird
should do *something*." A cheap win: on ambient-object
click, fire a tiny flavor message ("A sparrow — it
doesn't mind you.") without game-state change. Would
turn dead clicks into flavor beats.

### click 5 — a tile with a decorative dot

Couldn't target a specific dot reliably (chrome-mcp
coord precision issue), but code inspection shows these
dots are procedural — rendered from position-hashed
patterns in render.js, not stored as clickable
entities. A click lands on the tile, not the dot.

021 flagged this already ("tile-decoration dots have
no tooltips"). The kid-framing is stronger: **dots
look interactive (they're bright, small, irregular)
and they aren't**. A future fix wouldn't just add
tooltips — it'd need a data model that lets the render
pass emit click-targets.

## ideas generated → ideas.md

- **Widen citizen click hit-box** from 15px to 20-22px
  world-space. One-line change in `input.js:72`. Would
  make "I can see the citizen but can't click them"
  moments rare. (Actionable next-tick.)
- **Dead-click flavor beats**: when the click lands on
  a tile with nothing to select AND no build is
  active, fire a tiny flavor notify based on tile
  type. ("Mountain — impassable." / "Forest — lumber
  mill can harvest here.") Converts 10+ "click does
  nothing" moments per session into tiny engagement
  beats. Would need care not to spam.
- **Ambient-creature tap-flavor**: on click near an
  ambient creature, fire a one-line flavor note. ("A
  sparrow. It doesn't mind you.") Cheap, changes
  nothing mechanically, earns a grin.
- **Dots-as-click-targets** (promotes 021's idea):
  would require a tile→decoration data model change.
  Bigger scope than a tooltip fix.
- **Touch-device HUD discoverability**: the `title`
  attribute doesn't show on touch. If realm ever ships
  a touch build, need explicit long-press tooltip or
  always-visible labels. Filing for a future
  `the-mobile-reviewer` tick.
