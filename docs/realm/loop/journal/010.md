## real findings (new)

### 1. `[BLOCKING]` The danger toast asks for buildings the player cannot build

At Y1 D24 the toast reads: *"No defenders! Build walls,
barracks, or towers."*

None of those three buildings appear in the build bar. All
three exist in the `BUILDINGS` data (verified via
`./js/state.js` import: Barracks, Wall, Watch Tower). They're
tech-gated. But:

- `G.researchedTechs` is empty.
- No tutorial step ever mentioned research.
- The danger toast names buildings without saying "research
  X first."
- The Research button top-center is highlighted on game
  start but nothing pushes the player toward it.

**A fresh player sees the warning, scans the build bar,
finds nothing matching, panics.** This is a real onboarding
cliff. Novice tick (007) didn't surface it because novices
play Peaceful — Military is where the hole shows up.

**Prescriptive note for a future tick**: the danger toast
could detect whether the named buildings are currently
available to the player, and rephrase to *"Research
military_basics to unlock Barracks"* or similar. Alternatively,
auto-prepend the research step to the Military scenario's
tutorial.

### 2. Raids happen off-screen without ceremony

Pop dropped 6/7 → 6/3 with ☠️ 4 while time was advancing at
4× speed. The ticker shows the raid entries but I wasn't
watching. **For a gamer running on speed, the first real
consequence of losing a raid is just "citizens vanished."**
No pause, no screen flash I could catch, no specific "N
citizens died" alert in the toast channel — only the ticker
at bottom-left (which is easy to miss during active play).

A gamer with 30 years of city-builders would expect: game
auto-pauses on *first raid*, optional thereafter. Or at
minimum a sticky "⚠ Raid in progress" banner during combat
with a body-count summary afterward.

### 3. Survive-5-raids counter moves before any successful defense

By D24 the counter read 3/5. The player has built ZERO
military buildings, researched ZERO techs, produced ZERO
soldiers. Yet 3 "survived" raids are banked.

Possibilities:
- Game counts "raid fired, settlement not destroyed" as
  survived regardless of damage — lenient by design.
- Counter increments at the raid-warning phase, not the
  raid-result phase — bookkeeping bug.
- Raids on Hard + Military auto-trigger faster than
  citizens can die, so each raid only kills a handful,
  leaving the settlement technically "standing."

Either way, **the counter reads as a lie to the gamer** —
"surviving" a raid should require active resistance, or at
least a defense structure existing. If a settlement with no
soldiers, walls, or towers counts raids as survived, the
mission is passed by *standing still*.

### 4. `⚔ 2d` raid countdown is a good UX beat

Next to the kingdom name at the top: a small crossed-swords
icon + "2d" when a raid is imminent. Updates to 1d, 0d. It's
subtle, in peripheral vision, doesn't demand attention but
is scannable. **Rare-good example** of "urgent data in a
calm-looking chrome." Worth preserving if the codebase ever
refactors the HUD.

### 5. Tutorial is scenario-agnostic

Military scenario opens with *"Your settlers need food! Build
a Farm first."* — identical to Peaceful. For the Military
scenario specifically, a first step of *"Research military
basics — your barracks won't appear until you do"* would be
materially better.
