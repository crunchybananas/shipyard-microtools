## What this game is — candidate loglines

- A first-person mystery where a lighthouse keeper's grief became architecture: you wash up on an island whose living 1:240 model controls the world, and as you solve the keeper's puzzle-chain you realize you are walking a dead person's mourning ritual — and choosing, at the bottom, whether to descend into it forever or climb out and leave the light on for someone who will never return.
- ABYME is a recursion you can feel: every dive down lands you on the same beach gone a little more wrong — the coat off its hook, the footprints now yours, the window dark — until you find the cartographer one level below, still lit, still calling, and the real ending is the one verb the game never gave you: UP.
- A zero-asset Myst-lineage descent in which the model is the world and the world is a model: a meditation on whether the loop is a prison, a grief, or a message — wrapped around one irreversible choice (ring the bell or don't) and one final image you cannot un-see.

## Recommended direction

Build it as GRIEF with a vertical axis and a watching presence — Candidate Truth A, hardened by the Genre Historian's Keeper and the Skeptic/Art Director's "way out." The cartographer was the lighthouse keeper; they lost a partner and then watched the island itself begin to drown (the rising sea is why the valve exists — draining it is the keeper re-enacting the one day they could still hold the water back). Unable to let go, they built the living 1:240 model to keep a version where the light still turns and the water still obeys. But a model of a drowning island drowns too, so they built a smaller one inside it, and smaller again — each more perfect, more shrunken, more desperate. That is the recursion: not a flex, not a puzzle box, but a refusal rendered as geometry. The "far down, a light is still lit" pinprick is the keeper themselves at the bottom, still tending a lamp for someone who will never climb back up the stairs.

This resolves the central disagreements without flattening them. It makes the EXISTING texture load-bearing instead of orphaned: the coat that smells of "salt and lamp oil, still" becomes proof time does not pass inside the model (grief preserves) AND the ascent-reveal device ("it was always yours"); the maker's glyph (triangle + ringed dot) is re-read as a tiny self-portrait — island plus lamp — a man signing his own mourning, and it surfaces one last time in the constellation finale as the sender's mark; the footprints leading TO the model and never away are promoted from decoration to the Act-I mystery sentence; the warm window is the partner's window. The recursion stops being the reveal (the Art Director's correct objection) and becomes the MECHANIC; the reveal is the keeper and the choice.

It also fixes the structural reason 26 loops produced no story (the Skeptic's sharpest point): the loop optimized for "finished and verified," and graphics finish while story does not. So step one is a SPINE.md + a MISSION.md amendment that every story/puzzle tick must cite a named beat — graphics ticks can now be REJECTED for not serving an answered question. We keep the cozy aesthetic and then BREAK HEARTS with it (the Narrative Architect's tonal fork: A keeps warmth, B weaponizes it — we choose warmth-that-wounds, and reserve the betrayal register for the one "room that disagrees"). We give descent a direction by diverging every level (the beach below is WRONG by exactly one detail per the Finisher) and we give the game an UP (the Skeptic/Historian ascent) so the owner's literal question — what happens as we LEAVE — finally has an answer that is a designed act, not an absence.

## The One Truth — ★ CHOSEN: GRIEF → INTEGRATION

### ★ The deepening (the chosen arc) — grief is the descent; INTEGRATION is the exit

Grief (below) is the *setup*: the keeper's refusal rendered as nested geometry. But the chosen
reveal does not end in the keeper's two dead-end exits (loop forever / abandon the light). It ends
in a third act drawn from the integration truth — **you don't destroy the shadow or escape it, you
hold it.**

- **The figure at the bottom is the wounded self.** "The light still lit far down" — the keeper —
  is the part of you (the player, the keeper, the recursion's prisoner) that never got to heal. The
  descent through the nested, decaying islands is a descent through your own mirror-rooms toward it.
- **The true ending is integration, not escape.** At the bottom you neither ring-the-bell-forever
  nor climb out and let the light die. You *embrace* the keeper/child-self — and ascend carrying
  them, whole. The light does not go out; you are not stuck; you leave **changed.** This dissolves
  the panel's down-vs-up tension: **up, by integration.**
- **The partner waits above, not lost below.** Reframe "someone who will never climb the stairs"
  toward the *true companion who waits at the top, trusting you'll return* — shifting the feeling
  from irreversible loss toward reunion/wholeness. (Keep it spare and metaphoric.)
- **The diverging levels are emotional eras, not random decay** (see issue #13): each descent passes
  through a color-psychology stage — saturated gold → desaturated streetlight-green → a sickly
  false-gold → cold isolation-blue → golden-hour bittersweet. The grammar of a life, abstracted.
- **The final tone is bittersweet golden hour** — "some gardens grow even while the world burns."
  ABYME's golden grade + the constellation finale already reach for exactly this.

Everything below remains the grief mechanic and texture; this section is how it *resolves*.

### Grief — the descent (the mechanic and the setup)

GRIEF — the recursion is a person's refusal to let go. At the deepest reachable level (or the moment the player chooses to climb instead of dive) the player finds the cartographer: not a corpse and not a ghost, but the LAST, unfinished room — a model frozen mid-build, abandoned the instant completing it would have meant admitting the real island, and the person on it, were gone. The 'light still lit far down' is the keeper, and the final understanding is that every puzzle the player solved was a day the keeper used to relive. The coat is the reveal device: climbing out one level, the player finds it on the hook and realizes it is THEIRS — they are the next keeper, and the search has a shape, not an end. Final image (named, per the Skeptic's demand): the lamp left burning in an empty study, seen from the foot of the stairs as the player ascends past it — and then the constellation, now reading as a person left behind rather than a victory.

### Roads not taken (reference only — DO NOT BUILD; kept for context)

- QUARANTINE (Truth B, the uncanny inversion) — the model is not a memory but a containment cell. Something washed ashore once that must never leave; the only way to hold it was a recursive prison that feels like progress and delivers none. The maker's glyph is a warden's seal, the journal is the warden's log, and the gut-punch is that the puzzles the player solves are the player politely building their own next cell. The cartographer is GONE because they finished and left through the one door you never find. This weaponizes the cozy aesthetic instead of breaking your heart with it — choose this if the owner wants Phantasmagoria/7th-Guest dread over tenderness. It is mutually exclusive in TONE with Grief; do not blend.
- TRANSMISSION (Truth C, the cosmic reading) — the recursion runs the other way: the keeper built UPWARD and you are near the bottom. The whole structure is a signal trying to reach someone at the true top, and the lighthouse BEAM that writes glyphs is the literal payload. Completing the island writes the message onto YOU; leaving carries it one level up to the next person who washes ashore. This is the only truth where LEAVING is unambiguously the climax (directly answering the owner), and it justifies the richest new environments as 'other rooms of the transmitter' — but it leans least on existing texture and risks grandiosity over intimacy.
- WATCHED LOOP (Historian + Finisher hybrid, identity-collapse) — leave the metaphysics ambiguous and commit to the felt horror: the cartographer is the previous YOU, one loop up; the pinprick light is the next you, one loop down; the handwriting in the journal merges from the player's field notes into the keeper's. No grand 'why' is stated — the reveal is purely that the loop is inhabited by versions of the player, and the only question that matters is whether you climb over the drowning one below to get out. Cheapest to deliver diegetically (it rides onceKeys + the journal SVG pipeline) and the most replay-provoking, at the cost of a stated meaning.

## Leaving the island — the endgame (answers the owner's question)

Make LEAVING the true endgame and the mirror of the dive — the game currently has only DOWN, and that is the bug, not a detail. The sequence, built entirely from systems already shipped or cheaply extended:

> decay shipped in loops 30–32 was *unreachable* (`W.level` never exceeded 2 — the era-eras lived
> behind a debug flag). Re-ordered build queue: **(1) make `W.level` reachable + cost the dive
> [✓ iter 33] → (2) #14 Keeper voice + promote the figure [✓ iter 34] → (3) #22 finale fork + exempt
> the finale from `gradeBias` [✓ iter 35] → (4) #13 redux: fix `gradeBias` order-of-ops + re-author
> casts [✓ iter 36] → (5) #15 Keeper's Quarters [NEXT] → (6) #16/#17 abyss rooms → (7) #18/#24.** Do NOT build the abyss rooms (#16/#17) before the
> spine carries weight; do NOT re-tune casts in isolation; do NOT code the coat `visible:false` at L4+.

1) RECONTEXTUALIZE THE DIVE (small) — **✓ SHIPPED iter 33.** The brass-plate dive that just set
W.level = 2 is now a CHOICE with a cost: a two-touch *brink ritual* (first touch → the world goes
quiet via `A.duckAmbient`, the cost is named — 'The journal will not follow you down.' /
'The way back closes behind the light.'; step off the plate and it lets go; a second touch commits),
autosave pauses at the brink, and the dive now *increments* `W.level` to a capped `MAX_DEPTH` (4 for
now) — so the descent is **reachable** and **repeatable** for the first time. Still open: the bell
finale fires the old golden parade at depth (step 3), and there is no UP yet (step 4).

2) DIVERGE EACH LEVEL (medium). Drive the existing W.level integer into the grades and prop set so descent reads as decay, not a copy machine: a gradeBias(level) that desaturates/darkens the five master grades (no new hex — the Art Director's deep-via-existing-channels rule), the seeded mist ceiling rises so the fret never fully burns off by level 3, and one prop changes per level (coat on the hook → on the floor → gone; footprints out of the sea → into the sea; warm window lit → dark; the bell already faintly swinging). The chain stays solvable; the WORLD curdles. The bell's pitch drops a semitone per level as a HUD-free depth cue.

3) THE KEEPER, ONE LEVEL DOWN — **✓ SHIPPED iter 34** (the voice + the figure-looks-back; keying to chain flags vs depth can deepen later). The 'light still lit far down' gets a mouth — a band-limited synthesized voice keyed off the chain flags the game already tracks: valveTurned → 'Oh. Not again.'; rulerPlaced → 'You're faster than I was. Don't be proud of it.'; lensPlaced → 'When the beam writes, don't read all four.'; plumbHung → 'Step off the plate. I'm begging you.' He arcs curious → pleading → resigned. He is the first and only 'I/you' in the game.

4) BUILD THE WAY UP (large — the answer to the owner's question). In the lamp room, after the bell, the player can run the mechanism BACKWARD: the world SHRINKS 240× around them (the dive's swell math, inverted) and they rise OUT onto the chart table one level up — and realize the lighthouse island they woke on is itself a model on someone's table. Ascending COSTS what descending never did: each level up, a puzzle re-locks below, so you cannot yo-yo. As you pass the keeper going the other way, his voice falls silent behind you.

5) THE TWO TERMINAL STATES (medium — the Finisher's off-ramp, kept as a real fork the owner can choose to ship or collapse). **PARTIAL ✓ iter 35:** the RING-at-depth *tone* now forks — the surface bell keeps the golden parade, the deep bell withholds (held golden, no constellation, thinned score, the keeper murmurs; card "you keep the light now"), all exempt from the descent curdle. Still unbuilt: the DON'T-RING / CLIMB-OUT branch (needs the ascent #12). At the top, or at the bottom: RING THE BELL = acceptance/descent, the current finale (stems gather, constellation lights), now read as choosing the loop. DON'T RING / CLIMB OUT = the surf holds, the light far down goes OUT, the constellation never completes — escape as ABANDONMENT. The last whisper is his, fading: 'Don't leave the light on for me. I never could.' The title screen afterward reflects which world you left.

What it MEANS: the recursion was never a puzzle, it was the shape of a search. Down is the trap; up is the story; the choice between them is the only stake that isn't curiosity. Leaving doesn't escape the nest — it reveals you were always inside one too, and that someone is always still down there.

## New environments (zero-asset, palette-consistent)

### The Keeper's Quarters  *(medium)*

- **Mood:** warm, inhabited, mid-sentence — the hearth that makes every cold room frightening by contrast
- **Palette:** the ONE space with a local point-light at the lamp-oil color; grade biased toward golden/dawn warmth regardless of clock, all from existing channels
- **Narrative role:** The person the game has only implied. A cot, a cold stove, a calcified half-eaten meal, a chart in progress, the wall papered in nested island sketches shrinking toward a single dot — and the smallest sketch has the nestedGlint pinprick bleeding through the paper from behind. Converts the existing 'far down a light is still lit' secret into a narrative wound: the keeper KNEW where the descent led and drew themselves down it anyway.

### The Drowned Gallery  *(large)*

- **Mood:** claustrophobic, sunless, water-pressure dread — the first space with a ceiling you can touch and no horizon
- **Palette:** no sun; lit entirely by waterShallow caustics (the one warm-ish channel) against near-black fog walls — the proposed 'deep' grade built only from existing channels pushed to their limits
- **Narrative role:** When the tide valve drains, the chasm reveals a submerged stone corridor (reuse the cellar's BackSide box recipe at a low 2.2m ceiling), flooded to the knee as a function of W.tide. Teaches that draining is not safe — it OPENS things below — and reframes descent, not the 240x gimmick, as the real direction of the game.

### The Vault Beneath  *(large)*

- **Mood:** the sublime — awe at scale, the literal abyss the word 'abyme' demands
- **Palette:** deep grade; a far wall lit only at its base by waterShallow glow, the top lost in fog; drips that fall for a real two seconds as scale cues
- **Narrative role:** At the bottom of the Drowned Gallery, one vast inverted cone of rock with the next island's lighthouse hanging full-size and upside-down from the cave roof across black water (a modelAnchor clone). The first time the player SEES the recursion as architecture instead of a teleport cut — turning a clever transition into a vista of dread. Reuses the giant-ruler scale trick that already works.

### The Room That Disagrees  *(medium)*

- **Mood:** uncanny, trust-breaking — the cozy register betrayed in exactly one room
- **Palette:** the existing study cloned and lit cold (deep grade), one inverted assumption
- **Narrative role:** Reached only at depth: a second study identical to the first, but its chart-table model shows a WorldState the world is NOT in (sea drained when it's full; beam lit by day), and through its window is weather that contradicts yours. Give this room a private ghostState that ignores live W for the model only. The whole game taught the player to trust that the model tells the truth about the world; this room breaks that trust, and the break asks: which level is real, and am I the keeper or a thing someone else is turning?

### The Threshold (jetty + dory)  *(large)*

- **Mood:** departure, the promise of 'beyond' — the first exterior that is a destination, not decoration
- **Palette:** grade horizon at golden/night; wood/brass/cloth, all existing materials
- **Narrative role:** On the wash-up beach, a small jetty and beached boat that do nothing for most of the game — a standing promise. In the finale, rowing out gives the only shot looking BACK at the whole island as a silhouette; as you pull away it sits on a chart table and a hand reaches into frame. The horizon, decoration for 26 iterations, becomes the gesture of leaving — and proves the way out is the way in, one level up.

- **Phantasmagoria** — The slow wrongness of a familiar place; one perfectly-placed scare beats a hundred jump-scares  
  → The beach below diverges by exactly one detail per level (coat gone, footprints reversed, window dark). And one seeded dread beat: at night on a heavy-fret level >=3, leaning over the model, the tiny nestedGlint figure is standing and FACING the player's giant eye for two seconds, surf dropping out for a breath — then gone. The doll's house looks back.
- **Phantasmagoria** — Warm, beautiful surface concealing the dread underneath; a presence that was fine until you arrived  
  → Keep the cozy aesthetic 26 loops perfected, then break hearts with it (Grief) — or weaponize it (Quarantine). Either way the warmth is the setup, not the meal. The footprints-to-the-model, the warm window, the still-warm coat are the seeds the build let sit inert.
- **The Journeyman Project** — An irreversible CLOCK and a world that holds you accountable — nothing is ever free  
  → Bolt one consequence onto the existing deterministic time: the beam writes the dial code only in a narrow window, and the seeded weather can FOG OUT the cliff on a heavy-fret night, so on some nights the code is unreadable and you must wait/crank as drift accelerates per level. Plus one true point of no return: the plate dive becomes a committed choice with autosave stopped and the Keeper begging you to step off. The deterministic weather, currently used only for mood, becomes an adversary pointed at a puzzle.
- **Myst (the lineage thesis, not a clone)** — Recursion that conceals a HUMAN crime/grief — the Books and Ages only land because you learn WHO is trapped and WHY  
  → The 1:240 nesting must hide a person, not a diagram. Give it a why with an emotional answer (the keeper's grief/refusal), make the descent reveal the maker, and the recursion stops being a tech-demo victory lap and becomes the shape of someone's mourning.

## Live tensions (kept alive, not flattened — these are real forks)

- DOWN vs OUT — the Art Director and the Skeptic split on which is the real movement. The climax was built as diving DOWN; the owner asks about LEAVING (UP). The strongest synthesis fuses them (climbing out one level = the same act as diving down — you can never exit the nest), but the team must still decide whether the FINAL camera points at the seafloor or the horizon. Do not pretend this is settled.
- GRIEF vs QUARANTINE vs TRANSMISSION — the three Candidate Truths are mutually exclusive in TONE and cannot be hedged. The Narrative Architect is right that a story secretly all three is secretly none. Grief is cheapest (existing texture points there) and most certain; Quarantine best weaponizes the cozy look; Transmission best answers 'leaving' as climax. This is the owner's single highest-stakes call and the issue set must surface it, not decide it.
- COZY-STAYS vs COZY-BETRAYED — does the warm aesthetic remain a comfort that wounds (Grief), or get inverted into dread (Quarantine/Historian)? This governs every future graphics and audio tick; you cannot tune mood until it is chosen. Kept alive deliberately.
- CLOSED ENDING vs CHOICE — the Finisher and Architect disagree. One authored ending is stronger and cheaper; a fork (dive-down vs climb-out, ring vs don't-ring) answers 'leaving' more literally and adds replay but doubles finale work. Worth filing as an explicit decision rather than defaulting.
- SHOW THE CARTOGRAPHER vs KEEP THE LONELY PURITY — a glimpsed figure (silhouette in the warm window, the standing nestedGlint, the player's own coat) raises the emotional ceiling enormously but breaks the 'lonely island' framing and is the hardest zero-asset synthesis yet (a human form). In or out is unresolved among the personas.
- WHO IS THE PLAYER — stranger who washed up, the maker returning, or the next reader of the journal? The room-that-disagrees, the coat-reveal, and the ascent all hinge on this and the personas assume different answers. The story cannot have stakes until it is chosen, but the choice is genuinely open.
- PAUSE GRAPHICS vs KEEP POLISHING — multiple personas demand 2-3 story-only ticks and a MISSION.md rule that can REJECT a graphics tick for not advancing the thesis. This directly tensions against the owner's standing power-efficiency directive and the loop's proven habit of shipping easy graphics wins. Both pressures are legitimate; the resolution is the process issue, not a silent override.
